Data Protection Act 2018

UK Data Protection Act 2018 – domestic law implementing & supplementing UK GDPR

Overview

The **Data Protection Act 2018** (DPA 2018) is the primary UK domestic legislation governing personal data processing, supplementing the retained **UK GDPR** (post-Brexit version of EU GDPR) and implementing certain derogations permitted under EU GDPR. In March 2026, the DPA 2018 (as amended by the **Data Protection and Digital Information Act** 2024/2025) remains the cornerstone of UK data protection law, covering processing by public authorities, law enforcement (Part 3), national security (Part 4), and general processing (Part 2). Key updates include refined fines structure, ICO powers, research provisions, automated decision-making safeguards, cookie rules, international transfers, and streamlined compliance for SMEs. The Act applies to all UK organisations (and those offering goods/services to UK residents or monitoring behaviour), with strong alignment to EU GDPR (adequacy decision still in force), NIS2, DORA, and emerging AI regulation (EU AI Act, UK AI strategy).

Key Value: The DPA 2018 provides a clear, enforceable UK-specific framework for data protection — balancing innovation with individual rights, enabling international data flows (via UK GDPR adequacy), empowering the ICO, and ensuring compliance with UK-specific requirements (law enforcement, national security, research, electronic marketing) while maintaining high standards of privacy and accountability.

Core Elements of DPA 2018 (2026)

Part 2 – General Processing (UK GDPR)

  • Applies UK GDPR to most organisations
  • Derogations for research, archiving, journalism, national security
  • ICO enforcement & fines up to £17.5m / 4% global turnover

Part 3 – Law Enforcement Processing

  • Implementation of EU Law Enforcement Directive
  • Police, courts, prisons, immigration processing rules
  • Separate rights & safeguards

Part 4 – National Security Processing

  • Intelligence services & national security exemptions
  • Commissioner oversight & judicial review

Key Amendments (2024/2025)

  • Refined fines & ICO powers
  • Automated decision-making & profiling safeguards
  • Cookies & soft-opt-in rules
  • International data transfers & adequacy

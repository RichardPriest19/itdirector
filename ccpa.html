CCPA

California Consumer Privacy Act (as amended by CPRA) – foundational U.S. state privacy law granting consumer rights over personal information

Overview

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), effective 2020 and significantly expanded by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) in 2023, is the most comprehensive U.S. state privacy law. In March 2026, the California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA) fully enforces the CPRA framework, with finalized regulations (2024–2025) covering sensitive data categories, cybersecurity audits, risk assessments, automated decision-making, dark patterns prohibition, and opt-out preference signals. CCPA/CPRA applies to for-profit businesses meeting thresholds (>$25M revenue, handling 100,000+ consumers' data, or deriving 50%+ revenue from selling/sharing data) and grants California residents rights to know, delete, correct, opt out of sale/sharing, limit sensitive data use, and non-discrimination. Private right of action exists for data breaches, with record class actions and CPPA enforcement actions in 2025–2026.

Key Value: CCPA/CPRA compliance builds consumer trust, reduces litigation risk, aligns with global privacy standards, drives better data governance, and — when paired with Zero Trust — strengthens overall cybersecurity and privacy posture.

Core Consumer Rights & Obligations (2026)

Consumer Rights

  • Right to know/access (categories & specific pieces of PI)
  • Right to delete & correct personal information
  • Right to opt out of sale/sharing & limit sensitive data use
  • Right to non-discrimination & equal service

Business Obligations

  • Privacy notice & two-method opt-out mechanism
  • Respond to requests within 45 days (extendable)
  • Conduct cybersecurity audits & risk assessments (high-risk processors)
  • Prohibit dark patterns & honor Global Privacy Control (GPC)

Enforcement & Penalties

  • CPPA administrative fines up to $7,500 per intentional violation
  • Private right of action: $100–$750 per consumer per incident (breach)
  • Statutory damages & class actions common

Business Associate / Service Provider Requirements

  • Contracts limiting use to specified purposes
  • Downstream subcontractor obligations
  • Liability for processor violations

Key Enforcement & Trends in 2026

Cybersecurity & Risk Focus

  • Mandatory audits for high-risk data processing
  • Risk assessments & mitigation plans required
  • Alignment with NIST & Zero Trust practices

Sensitive Data & Opt-Out

  • Expanded sensitive PI categories & use limitations
  • Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal enforcement
  • Dark pattern prohibition & user experience scrutiny

Litigation & Private Actions

  • Increased class actions for breaches & opt-out failures
  • CPPA rulemaking on automated decision-making & profiling
  • Coordination with other state laws (VCDPA, CTDPA, etc.)

Best Practices for CCPA/CPRA Compliance (2026)

Data Mapping & Governance

  • Maintain comprehensive data inventory & flow mapping
  • Implement data minimization & retention policies
  • Use automated tools for request intake & fulfillment

Security & Technical Controls

  • Enforce phishing-resistant MFA & encryption
  • Conduct regular risk assessments & audits
  • Implement Zero Trust & SSPM for SaaS vendors

Consumer Experience & Training

  • Clear privacy notice & easy opt-out mechanisms
  • Annual workforce training & vendor oversight
  • Test request handling & dark pattern avoidance

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion CCPA/CPRA as core U.S. privacy compliance program
  • Approve risk assessments, security controls & vendor contracts
  • Ensure Zero Trust & phishing-resistant security across systems
  • Oversee request fulfillment, breach response & audit readiness
  • Monitor CPPA enforcement, litigation trends & fine exposure
  • Drive workforce training & third-party risk management
  • Plan for alignment with emerging state & federal privacy laws

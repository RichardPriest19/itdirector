SIEM

Security Information and Event Management – centralized log collection, correlation, threat detection & response

Overview

Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) is the foundational technology for collecting, aggregating, correlating, and analyzing security event and log data from across the enterprise to detect threats, generate alerts, support investigations, and meet compliance requirements. In March 2026, SIEM has matured into **AI-powered**, **cloud-native**, **open-telemetry-friendly** platforms with strong convergence toward **SOAR** (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response), **UEBA** (User and Entity Behavior Analytics), **threat hunting**, and **business-context correlation**. Leading solutions (Microsoft Sentinel, Splunk Enterprise Security, Elastic Security, Google Chronicle, Exabeam, Sumo Logic, IBM QRadar, LogRhythm) emphasize **low-fidelity alert reduction**, **automated investigation**, **real-time analytics**, **multi-cloud/hybrid ingestion**, **compliance mapping** (GDPR, PCI-DSS, HIPAA, NIS2, SOC 2), and integration with EDR, XDR, SOAR, and identity platforms.

Key Value: SIEM provides centralized visibility into security events, enables early threat detection, accelerates incident response, reduces alert fatigue through AI, supports compliance & audit requirements, and — when integrated with SOAR & XDR — forms the backbone of modern SOC operations.

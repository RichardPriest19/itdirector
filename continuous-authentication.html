Continuous Authentication

Real-time, risk-based re-verification of identity and access beyond initial login – a core pillar of mature Zero Trust

Overview

Continuous (or adaptive) authentication evaluates context, behavior, device posture, and risk signals throughout a session, stepping up or down authentication requirements dynamically.

Core Components

  • Behavioral biometrics and anomaly detection
  • Device health and posture checks
  • Location, network, and time-based signals
  • Seamless step-up MFA when risk increases

Relation to Zero Trust

Eliminates implicit trust after initial login. In 2026, leading IAM platforms combine continuous authentication with ZTNA for truly identity-centric security.

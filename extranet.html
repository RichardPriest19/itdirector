Extranet

Secure, controlled external network that allows authorized third parties (partners, suppliers, customers, contractors) to access specific internal resources and collaborate safely

Overview

An extranet is a private network that uses internet technology and public telecommunications to securely share part of an organization’s internal information or operations with external users such as business partners, suppliers, vendors, or customers. It sits between the public internet and the fully internal intranet.

Key Characteristics

  • Controlled access using authentication (SSO, MFA, certificates)
  • Granular permissions and role-based access control
  • Encrypted communication (TLS, VPN, Zero Trust models)
  • Segregated from both the public internet and internal intranet
  • Audit logging and monitoring of all external activity

Common Use Cases

  • Partner and supplier portals for collaboration and data exchange
  • Customer self-service portals and B2B ordering systems
  • Project collaboration with external consultants or contractors
  • Regulatory reporting and secure document exchange
  • Dealer/distributor extranets in manufacturing and retail

Modern Extranet Architecture (2026)

Traditional VPN-based extranets are increasingly being replaced or supplemented by Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA), secure service edges (SASE), and cloud-native platforms. Modern extranets emphasize least-privilege access, continuous verification, and seamless integration with Microsoft 365, Salesforce, and other enterprise systems.

IT Director Considerations

Key challenges include balancing usability with security, managing third-party risk, ensuring compliance with data protection regulations, and maintaining clear segregation between internal and external environments.

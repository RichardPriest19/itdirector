Sovereign Cloud / Geopatriation

Dedicated cloud environments ensuring data residency, regulatory compliance, and geopolitical resilience

Overview

Sovereign Cloud provides isolated, jurisdiction-specific infrastructure operated under local laws, often with government oversight or certified operators. It addresses data sovereignty, supply-chain risks, and geopolitical tensions driving "geopatriation" strategies in 2026.

Key Drivers in 2026

  • EU data residency and EU AI Act requirements
  • National security concerns and restrictions on foreign cloud providers
  • Critical infrastructure protection (NIS2, DORA)
  • Supply-chain diversification away from single hyperscalers

Major Offerings

  • AWS EU Sovereign Cloud, Microsoft Azure Sovereign Clouds
  • National clouds (e.g., France SecNumCloud, Germany C5)
  • Hybrid models with local operators and attestation

Strategic Considerations

Evaluate workload placement based on sensitivity, latency needs, and compliance. Sovereign clouds often carry a 10-30% cost premium but reduce regulatory and geopolitical risk significantly.

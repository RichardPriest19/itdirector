ISO/IEC 42001

ISO/IEC 42001 – requirements for an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) (2023 edition)

Overview

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the world's first international standard specifying requirements and providing guidance for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). It helps organizations manage AI-related risks and opportunities responsibly throughout the AI lifecycle. In March 2026, ISO/IEC 42001 is the globally recognized benchmark for responsible AI governance, increasingly used for certification, compliance with the EU AI Act (high-risk systems), enterprise AI policies, public procurement, investor due diligence, and alignment with GDPR, NIST AI Risk Management Framework, and other privacy/ethics guidelines. It introduces AI-specific controls (Annex A), risk treatment, impact assessments, transparency, fairness, robustness, accountability, and data governance, and is certifiable as a standalone management system or integrated with ISO 27001 (ISMS) or ISO 27701 (PIMS).

Key Value: ISO/IEC 42001 provides a certifiable, risk-based framework for responsible AI adoption — helping organizations manage ethical, legal, technical, and societal risks, demonstrate accountability, build stakeholder trust, meet emerging regulatory obligations (EU AI Act, etc.), and scale AI safely and sustainably.

Core Requirements & Controls (2023 Edition – 2026)

AIMS Structure & Clauses

  • Context of the organization & interested parties
  • Leadership & commitment (AI policy, roles)
  • Planning (risks/opportunities, objectives, AI impact assessment)
  • Support (resources, competence, awareness, communication)
  • Operation (AI system lifecycle controls)
  • Performance evaluation & improvement

Annex A – AI-Specific Controls (new in 42001)

  • A.2 – Policies related to AI (ethics, transparency)
  • A.3 – Internal organization (AI governance roles)
  • A.4 – Resources for AI systems (data, compute, talent)
  • A.5 – AI system impact assessment
  • A.6 – AI system lifecycle (design, development, deployment, monitoring)
  • A.7 – Third-party relationships & supply chain
  • A.8 – Incident management & AI system improvement

Integration & Alignment

  • Compatible & integrable with ISO 27001, 27701, 42001
  • Supports EU AI Act high-risk system obligations
  • Aligns with NIST AI RMF, OECD AI Principles

Key Adoption & Trends in 2026

Certification & Regulatory

  • Certifiable AIMS standard (first certifications growing rapidly)
  • Strong mapping to EU AI Act high-risk & systemic risk obligations
  • Referenced in public procurement, investor ESG due diligence

Responsible AI & Ethics

  • Focus on fairness, bias mitigation, transparency, explainability
  • Privacy & data governance in AI (link to ISO 27701)
  • Sustainability & environmental impact of AI systems

Emerging AI Risks

  • Controls for generative AI & large language models
  • Supply chain & third-party AI provider risks
  • Integration with Zero Trust & secure AI development

Best Practices for ISO/IEC 42001 Adoption (2026)

AIMS Implementation

  • Extend existing ISO 27001/27701 system with AI scope
  • Conduct AI impact & risk assessments
  • Define AI policy, roles & ethical guidelines

Control Selection & Evidence

  • Select Annex A controls based on AI risk profile
  • Implement AI by design & default principles
  • Maintain evidence (policies, assessments, audit logs)

Continuous Improvement

  • Regular AIMS audits & management reviews
  • Update for emerging AI risks (gen AI, multimodal)
  • Integrate with incident, change & supplier processes

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion ISO/IEC 42001 as core responsible AI governance framework
  • Approve AIMS scope, policy & certification roadmap
  • Ensure AI risk & impact assessments are conducted
  • Oversee ethical guidelines, transparency & accountability
  • Monitor compliance mapping (EU AI Act, GDPR, etc.)
  • Drive AI by design & supply chain controls
  • Align AIMS with ISO 27001 ISMS & Zero Trust strategy

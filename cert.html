CERT

CERT – SEI/Carnegie Mellon security designation covering incident response, vulnerability management, and cybersecurity resilience

Overview

CERT (Computer Emergency Response Team) is a designation originating from the Software Engineering Institute (SEI) at Carnegie Mellon University. It refers both to the SEI’s CERT Division — a world-leading cybersecurity research and training organisation — and to the broader global network of national and organisational CERTs. SEI CERT training programmes equip professionals with skills in incident response, vulnerability analysis, insider threat management, and enterprise resilience.

Key Value: CERT training and designations from SEI represent the gold standard in incident response and vulnerability management education, underpinning national cybersecurity programmes and enterprise security operations worldwide.

Core CERT Training Areas

Incident Response & Handling

  • Apply structured incident response methodologies — preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery, and lessons learned
  • Operate within a CERT/CSIRT team structure with defined roles, escalation paths, and communication protocols
  • Conduct post-incident analysis to improve detection, response, and resilience capabilities
  • Coordinate with national CERTs, sector-specific CERTs, and law enforcement during major incidents

Vulnerability Analysis & Management

  • Identify, triage, and prioritise vulnerabilities using CVSS scoring and threat context
  • Coordinate responsible disclosure with vendors and publish security advisories
  • Integrate vulnerability intelligence into patch management and risk remediation workflows
  • Track vulnerability trends and emerging threat patterns through structured analysis

Insider Threat Management

  • Detect and respond to insider threats using behavioural analytics and technical controls
  • Apply the CERT Insider Threat Framework to organisational risk assessment
  • Design people, process, and technology countermeasures for insider threat mitigation

CSIRT & SOC Operations

  • Establish and mature a Computer Security Incident Response Team (CSIRT) or SOC capability
  • Define service catalogues, SLAs, escalation procedures, and stakeholder communication plans
  • Implement threat intelligence sharing using STIX/TAXII and ISACs

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Establish or contract a CERT/CSIRT capability with defined scope, authority, and escalation paths
  • Register with national CERT organisations (e.g., NCSC in the UK) and participate in threat intelligence sharing
  • Ensure incident response plans align with CERT best practices and are tested regularly
  • Train IT staff in CERT-aligned incident handling procedures and reporting protocols

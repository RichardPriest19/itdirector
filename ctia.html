CTIA

Certified Threat Intelligence Analyst – EC-Council certification for cyber threat intelligence collection, analysis, and dissemination

Overview

The Certified Threat Intelligence Analyst (CTIA) is awarded by EC-Council and is designed for cybersecurity professionals who collect, analyse, and apply threat intelligence to improve defensive operations. It covers the full threat intelligence lifecycle — from data collection and processing through analysis, production, and dissemination — enabling analysts to deliver actionable intelligence to SOC, incident response, and executive stakeholders.

Key Value: CTIA equips analysts with a structured, methodology-driven approach to threat intelligence that goes beyond raw data collection — producing intelligence products that directly inform risk decisions, detection improvements, and proactive threat hunting.

Core Exam Domains

Threat Intelligence Fundamentals

  • Understand the threat intelligence lifecycle — direction, collection, processing, analysis, dissemination, and feedback
  • Differentiate between strategic, operational, tactical, and technical intelligence
  • Apply threat intelligence frameworks — Cyber Kill Chain, Diamond Model, and MITRE ATT&CK
  • Understand threat actor categories — nation-state, cybercriminal, hacktivist, and insider threats

Data Collection & Processing

  • Collect threat data from OSINT, dark web, HUMINT, commercial feeds, and ISAC sharing
  • Process and normalise raw data into structured intelligence using STIX/TAXII formats
  • Operate threat intelligence platforms (TIPs) including Anomali, ThreatConnect, and MISP
  • Manage intelligence source reliability and information credibility ratings

Intelligence Analysis

  • Apply structured analytic techniques — ACH, kill chain analysis, and diamond model analysis
  • Perform malware analysis, IOC extraction, and TTPs mapping to MITRE ATT&CK
  • Conduct threat actor profiling, campaign attribution, and strategic threat assessment
  • Identify intelligence gaps and formulate priority intelligence requirements (PIRs)

Dissemination & Feedback

  • Produce intelligence reports tailored to strategic, operational, and tactical audiences
  • Share intelligence through ISACs, STIX/TAXII, and bilateral sharing agreements
  • Integrate intelligence into SIEM detection rules, vulnerability priorities, and incident response

Exam Details

  • Exam code: 312-85 — 50 multiple-choice questions over 2 hours
  • Prerequisite: 2 years of cybersecurity or intelligence experience
  • Renewal: 120 EC-Council Continuing Education (ECE) credits every 3 years

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Deploy CTIA-qualified professionals in threat intelligence, SOC, and incident response functions
  • Establish a threat intelligence programme with defined consumers, PIRs, and production workflows
  • Integrate threat intelligence into SIEM, firewall, EDR, and vulnerability management tooling
  • Use CTIA intelligence products to inform risk reporting, security investment, and board-level briefings

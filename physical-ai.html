Physical AI / Embodied AI

AI integrated with robotics, sensors, and real-world interaction – bridging digital intelligence with physical operations

Overview

Physical AI (also called Embodied AI) enables systems to perceive, reason about, and act in the physical world using sensors, actuators, and real-time data. Gartner lists it among the top strategic technology trends for 2026, with applications in manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, and autonomous systems.

Core Technologies

  • Digital twins synchronized with physical assets
  • Multimodal AI combining vision, language, and proprioception
  • Edge computing for low-latency decision making
  • Human-robot collaboration and safety systems

Enterprise Use Cases

  • Smart factories and predictive maintenance
  • Autonomous warehouses and last-mile delivery
  • AI-assisted surgery and eldercare robotics
  • OT/ICS environments with enhanced security

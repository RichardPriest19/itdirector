ISO/IEC/IEEE 12207

ISO/IEC/IEEE 12207 – international standard for software life cycle processes (2017 edition)

Overview

ISO/IEC/IEEE 12207:2017 (harmonized with ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288:2015) is the current international standard defining a common framework for software life cycle processes. It describes processes, activities, and tasks used during the acquisition, supply, development, operation, maintenance, and disposal of software products and services. In March 2026, ISO/IEC/IEEE 12207:2017 remains the globally accepted reference for structured software engineering processes — process-agnostic and tailorable to support waterfall, agile, DevOps, hybrid, and emerging AI/ML software development lifecycles. It is widely used in government, defense, aerospace, regulated industries (medical devices, automotive, finance), and enterprise software governance, often as a basis for process improvement, supplier contracts, compliance (CMMI, ISO 9001, FDA 21 CFR Part 11), and alignment with ITIL 4, ISO 27001, and supply chain security requirements.

Key Value: ISO/IEC/IEEE 12207 provides a comprehensive, tailorable, internationally recognized framework for managing the full software life cycle — improving consistency, quality, traceability, risk management, and supplier control while supporting modern agile/DevOps practices and emerging AI/software supply chain governance.

ISO/IEC 27701

ISO/IEC 27701 – extension to ISO 27001 for privacy information management system (PIMS) requirements & controls (2019 edition)

Overview

ISO/IEC 27701:2019 is the international standard that specifies requirements and provides guidance for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a Privacy Information Management System (PIMS). It extends ISO/IEC 27001 (ISMS) and ISO/IEC 27002 with privacy-specific controls and requirements for both PII controllers and PII processors. In March 2026, ISO/IEC 27701 remains the leading certifiable global privacy management standard, widely used to demonstrate GDPR compliance (Art. 24, 32, 42), align with CCPA/CPRA, LGPD, PDPA, APPI, and other privacy laws, and manage third-party/privacy risks. It introduces two new roles (PII Controller, PII Processor) and adds 31 new controls + guidance to Annex A of ISO 27001, with strong focus on privacy by design/default, data minimization, transparency, and accountability.

Key Value: ISO/IEC 27701 provides a certifiable, risk-based privacy management framework that integrates seamlessly with existing ISO 27001 ISMS, demonstrates privacy accountability, supports global regulatory compliance, reduces privacy risks, and builds trust with customers, regulators, and partners.

Core Requirements & Controls (2019 Edition – 2026)

PIMS Roles & Scope

  • PII Controller – determines purposes & means of processing
  • PII Processor – processes PII on behalf of controller
  • Extension to ISO 27001 scope (includes privacy risks)

Key Additional Controls (Annex A)

  • A.7.2.1 – PII controller obligations (consent, transparency)
  • A.7.3.7 – PII processor obligations (sub-processor management)
  • A.8.2.1 – Data minimization & purpose limitation
  • A.8.5.1 – Privacy by design & default
  • A.8.5.2 – PII retention & disposal

Management System Requirements

  • Privacy risk assessment & treatment
  • Privacy impact assessments (PIAs/DPIAs)
  • Records of processing activities (RoPA)
  • Incident response & breach notification

Key Adoption & Trends in 2026

Certification & Compliance

  • Certifiable extension to ISO 27001 (PIMS certification)
  • Strong mapping to GDPR accountability & Art. 32 security
  • Referenced in NIS2, DORA, CCPA/CPRA, LGPD compliance

Privacy Risk & Third-Party

  • Focus on supply-chain & processor obligations
  • Integration with vendor risk & contract management
  • Privacy by design in AI/ML & cloud workloads

Emerging Privacy Challenges

  • Controls for AI/ML processing of PII
  • Alignment with Zero Trust & multi-cloud privacy
  • Support for automated privacy assessments

Best Practices for ISO/IEC 27701 Adoption (2026)

PIMS Implementation

  • Extend existing ISO 27001 ISMS with privacy scope
  • Conduct privacy risk assessment & DPIAs
  • Document RoPA & maintain privacy notices

Control Selection & Evidence

  • Select privacy-specific controls from Annex A
  • Implement privacy by design & default
  • Maintain evidence (policies, consents, audit logs)

Continuous Improvement

  • Regular PIMS audits & management reviews
  • Update for emerging privacy risks (AI, supply chain)
  • Integrate with incident & change processes

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion ISO/IEC 27701 as core privacy management standard
  • Approve PIMS scope extension & certification roadmap
  • Ensure privacy risk assessments & DPIAs
  • Oversee RoPA, privacy notices & third-party obligations
  • Monitor compliance mapping (GDPR, CCPA, LGPD, etc.)
  • Drive privacy by design & AI risk controls
  • Align PIMS with ISO 27001 ISMS & Zero Trust strategy

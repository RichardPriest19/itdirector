ESXi

VMware ESXi – bare-metal Type-1 hypervisor for enterprise virtualization

Overview

VMware ESXi is the leading bare-metal (Type-1) hypervisor that enables organizations to run multiple virtual machines and workloads on a single physical server with high performance, strong isolation, and enterprise-grade features. In March 2026, **ESXi 8.0 Update 3+** is the current production standard, with **ESXi 9.0** in preview/early access. It remains dominant in on-premises, hybrid, and edge environments — powering **vSphere 8.0 / 9.0** clusters, **vSphere with Tanzu** for Kubernetes-native workloads, **vSphere Distributed Services Engine** (DPU/smartNIC offload), **AI/ML acceleration** (vGPU, NVIDIA AI Enterprise), **zero-trust security model**, **broad hardware support** (AMD EPYC, Intel Xeon, Arm64), **edge & ROBO** deployments, and **subscription licensing** transition. ESXi integrates tightly with **VMware Aria Suite**, **Tanzu**, **NSX**, **VCF (VMware Cloud Foundation)**, **Broadcom ecosystem**, and third-party tools (Dell VxRail, HPE GreenLake, Lenovo ThinkAgile).

Key Value: ESXi delivers rock-solid, high-performance, secure virtualization — enabling massive consolidation, workload portability, zero-trust isolation, Kubernetes integration, AI/ML acceleration, edge & ROBO support, and — with vSphere & Tanzu — providing a unified platform for traditional VMs, modern cloud-native apps, and hybrid cloud operations.

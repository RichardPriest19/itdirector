GAAP

Generally Accepted Accounting Principles – US financial reporting framework

Overview

GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles) is the standard framework of guidelines, rules, and procedures used in the United States for financial accounting and reporting. Issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB) and codified in the Accounting Standards Codification (ASC), GAAP ensures consistency, comparability, transparency, and reliability in financial statements. In March 2026, GAAP remains the primary standard for US public companies, private entities requiring GAAP-compliant statements, and many international organizations with US investors, listings, or financing. It is under increased scrutiny due to **SEC sustainability & climate disclosure rules**, **human capital & pay equity reporting**, **convergence efforts with IFRS** (revenue recognition ASC 606, leases ASC 842, financial instruments), **AI/ML in financial reporting** (automation, anomaly detection, forecasting), **ESG integration** (climate-related disclosures, Scope 1–3 emissions), **cyber risk quantification** in footnotes, and **regulatory pressure** (SEC climate rules, EU CSRD influence, DORA financial resilience). IT plays a critical role in **GAAP-compliant systems** (ERP, consolidation tools), **data governance** for ESG/human capital metrics, **audit trails**, **AI-assisted compliance checks**, and **real-time reporting**.

Key Value: GAAP provides a consistent, transparent, and reliable financial reporting framework — ensuring comparability for investors & regulators, supporting defensible financial statements, enabling accurate valuation & risk assessment, facilitating compliance with SEC & other mandates, and — when integrated with modern ERP & AI tools — delivering real-time visibility, automated controls, ESG/human capital integration, and reduced reporting risk in a highly regulated environment.

Core GAAP Areas & IT Relevance (2026)

Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)

  • Five-step model for revenue from contracts with customers
  • IT role: ERP configuration, contract management, deferred revenue

Leases (ASC 842)

  • Right-of-use assets & lease liabilities on balance sheet
  • IT role: lease management software, amortization schedules

Financial Instruments & Consolidation

  • ASC 820 (fair value), ASC 810 (consolidation)
  • IT role: multi-entity consolidation tools, real-time reporting

ESG & Emerging Disclosures

  • SEC climate rules & human capital disclosure
  • IT role: ESG data collection, AI validation, footnote automation

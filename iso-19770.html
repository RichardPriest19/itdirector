ISO/IEC 19770 family

ISO/IEC 19770 – international standards family for IT asset management systems & practices

Overview

The ISO/IEC 19770 family defines requirements, guidance, and best practices for IT asset management (ITAM), including software asset management (SAM), hardware asset management, and emerging areas (AI/ML assets). The core standard is **ISO/IEC 19770-1:2017** (requirements for an IT asset management system), which is certifiable and specifies processes for identification, control, reporting, and optimization of IT assets throughout their lifecycle. In March 2026, the family includes **19770-2:2017** (software ID tags), **19770-3:2017** (entitlement schema), **19770-4:2017** (utilization measurement), **19770-5:2015** (overview & vocabulary), **19770-6:2023** (hardware asset management), and **19770-8:2023** (machine learning & AI asset management). It is widely adopted in enterprises, government, and managed service providers for license compliance, cost optimization, risk reduction, audit readiness, and alignment with ISO 27001, ITIL 4, GDPR, SOX, and NIS2.

Key Value: The ISO/IEC 19770 family provides a certifiable, structured framework for managing IT assets — reducing software license costs, mitigating compliance & security risks, improving visibility & control, and enabling accurate reporting and audit defense across hardware, software, cloud, and emerging AI/ML assets.

Core Standards & Requirements (2026)

ISO/IEC 19770-1:2017 – ITAM System Requirements

  • Management responsibility & policy
  • Asset identification, control & lifecycle management
  • Risk & performance evaluation
  • Continual improvement & management review

Supporting Parts

  • 19770-2 – Software identification (SWID) tags
  • 19770-3 – Software entitlement schema
  • 19770-4 – Resource utilization measurement
  • 19770-6 – Hardware asset management
  • 19770-8 – Machine learning & AI asset management

Integration & Alignment

  • Compatible with ISO 27001 (integrated ITAM + ISMS)
  • Supports ITIL 4 asset & configuration management
  • Aligns with GDPR, NIS2, SOX, FedRAMP compliance

Key Adoption & Trends in 2026

Certification & Compliance

  • Certifiable ITAM system (19770-1)
  • Increasing use in software license audits & compliance
  • Referenced in government procurement & MSP contracts

AI & Emerging Assets

  • 19770-8:2023 – specific guidance for ML/AI assets
  • Focus on model governance, data lineage & compute resources
  • Integration with AI ethics (ISO 42001) & privacy (ISO 27701)

Cloud & Hybrid Environments

  • Support for SaaS, IaaS, PaaS asset tracking
  • Alignment with ISO 27017/27018 cloud controls
  • Multi-cloud visibility & license optimization

Cyber Essentials Plus

Enhanced UK cybersecurity certification with technical verification and annual assessments

Overview

Cyber Essentials Plus is the enhanced level of the UK government-backed Cyber Essentials scheme. While standard Cyber Essentials relies on organization self-assessment, Cyber Essentials Plus requires independent technical verification by NCSC-approved assessors. This rigorous assessment provides significant credibility and assurance that the five core security controls are properly implemented and effective. Organizations pursuing government contracts, working with sensitive data, or requiring high customer confidence increasingly opt for Cyber Essentials Plus certification.

Key Advantage: Cyber Essentials Plus provides independent third-party verification of security controls, giving customers, partners, and regulators high confidence in your security posture. Essential for government suppliers and high-security requirements.

Cyber Essentials Plus vs Standard Cyber Essentials

Standard Cyber Essentials

  • Assessment: Self-assessment questionnaire (SAQ) only
  • Verification: Organization completes checklist; no independent verification
  • Scope: Questionnaire only
  • On-Site Visit: No
  • Technical Testing: No
  • Cost: Lower (£100-300)
  • Credibility: Self-assessed compliance
  • Timeline: 1-2 months to certification

Cyber Essentials Plus

  • Assessment: Independent technical assessment by NCSC-approved assessor
  • Verification: Third-party assessor tests and verifies all five controls
  • Scope: Comprehensive documentation and technical evaluation
  • On-Site Visit: Yes—assessor visits organization
  • Technical Testing: Extensive testing and validation
  • Cost: Higher (£1,000-5,000+)
  • Credibility: High credibility with external stakeholders
  • Timeline: 2-3 months to certification

The Five Controls (Assessed in Detail)

Cyber Essentials Plus verifies the same five core controls as standard Cyber Essentials, but with much greater depth and technical rigor:

1. Boundary Firewalls and Internet Gateways (Technical Assessment)

Assessor verifies firewall effectiveness through:

  • Network diagram review and validation
  • Firewall configuration inspection
  • Port scanning and connectivity testing
  • Verification of rule blocking unnecessary traffic
  • Testing for exposed services and vulnerabilities
  • Log review for monitoring effectiveness
  • Assessment of redundancy and failover

2. Secure Configuration (Technical Assessment)

Assessor verifies system hardening through:

  • System inventory and baseline comparison
  • Operating system configuration review
  • Verification of disabled unnecessary services
  • Testing for default credentials
  • Security setting verification
  • Software inventory and patch level
  • Physical inspection of devices and equipment

3. User Access Control (Testing and Verification)

Assessor verifies access controls through:

  • User account inventory and role verification
  • Testing unauthorized access attempts
  • Privilege escalation testing
  • Verification of admin account restrictions
  • Access control testing (can user access systems they shouldn't?)
  • Review of segregation of duties
  • Testing for weak or reused passwords

4. Malware Protection (Verification and Testing)

Assessor verifies malware protection through:

  • Inventory of protected systems
  • Verification of anti-malware deployment
  • Definition update verification
  • Real-time scanning verification
  • Testing with non-malicious malware samples (EICAR test files)
  • Review of quarantine and remediation logs
  • Assessment of excluded systems

5. Patch Management (Scanning and Verification)

Assessor verifies patch management through:

  • System vulnerability scanning across all systems
  • Identification of missing security patches
  • Assessment of patching timeliness
  • Review of patch management process and records
  • Testing systems for known vulnerabilities
  • Verification of firmware updates
  • Assessment of excluded systems and justification

Cyber Essentials Plus Assessment Process

Stage 1: Scoping and Documentation Review

  • Duration: 1-2 weeks before on-site visit
  • Assessor reviews organization information
  • Scoping discussion: What systems are included?
  • Documentation review of policies and procedures
  • Planning of on-site assessment activities

Stage 2: Pre-Assessment Activities

  • Duration: 1-2 weeks before visit
  • Network scanning for vulnerabilities
  • Systems inventory compilation
  • Test planning and preparation
  • Schedule confirmation with organization

Stage 3: On-Site Assessment

  • Duration: Typically 1-3 days on-site
  • Activities:
    • Network architecture walkthrough
    • System and device inspection
    • Firewall configuration review
    • Access control testing
    • Physical security inspection
    • Staff interviews (IT staff, security, management)
    • Hands-on testing of systems
  • Evidence Collection:
    • Screenshots of configurations
    • Test results and logs
    • Policy and procedure verification
    • Vulnerability scan results

Stage 4: Post-Assessment Analysis

  • Duration: 1-2 weeks after visit
  • Detailed analysis of findings
  • Report compilation
  • Identification of any non-conformities
  • Preparation of assessment report

Stage 5: Assessment Report and Certification Decision

  • Report issued with findings
  • If compliant: Certification issued valid for 12 months
  • If non-conformities found:
    • Major non-conformities must be remediated
    • Re-assessment may be required
    • Organization has 30 days to provide evidence of remediation

Stage 6: Annual Reassessment

  • Annual reassessment required for certification renewal
  • Similar process to initial assessment
  • Typically quicker (focus on changes and high-risk areas)
  • Certificate renewed for another 12 months

Types of Assessment Findings

Non-Conformities

Issues that prevent certification:

  • Critical Non-Conformities: Control not implemented or severely ineffective. Must be remediated before certification.
  • Major Non-Conformities: Significant gaps in control implementation. Must be remediated, typically within 30 days.
  • Minor Non-Conformities: Small gaps or areas for improvement. Should be addressed; may not prevent certification.

Observations

Recommendations for improvement:

  • Best practice suggestions
  • Areas for optimization
  • Security enhancements beyond core controls
  • Not required for certification but valuable for improving posture

Cyber Essentials Plus Assessment Costs

Direct Costs

  • Assessment Fee: £1,000-£5,000+ depending on organization size and complexity
  • Variation Factors:
    • Number of systems in scope
    • Geographical spread
    • Complexity of IT environment
    • Assessor travel requirements
    • Additional testing needs

Indirect Costs

  • IT staff time for assessment preparation and support
  • Tools and systems needed to remediate findings
  • Process and procedure documentation
  • Annual reassessment costs

Cost Justification

While more expensive than standard Cyber Essentials, the benefits justify the cost for many organizations:

  • Government contract eligibility and competitive advantage
  • Customer confidence and competitive differentiation
  • Risk reduction from verified security controls
  • Regulatory compliance demonstration
  • Identified vulnerabilities preventing costly breaches

Approved Assessors

NCSC-Approved Assessors

Only NCSC-approved assessors can conduct Cyber Essentials Plus assessments. These organizations have demonstrated:

  • Technical expertise and knowledge
  • Assessment methodology competency
  • Impartiality and independence
  • Professional conduct standards
  • Adherence to NCSC procedures

Finding an Assessor

  • NCSC website lists approved assessor organizations
  • Multiple assessors available (competition is good)
  • Get quotes from multiple assessors
  • Consider assessor experience and expertise
  • Ensure compatibility with your organization's needs

Preparation for Cyber Essentials Plus Assessment

Pre-Assessment Planning (2-3 months before)

  • Define clear scope (systems and locations)
  • Conduct internal security assessment
  • Identify and remediate obvious gaps
  • Gather and organize documentation
  • Brief IT staff on assessment process
  • Prepare network and system information

Control Implementation (1-2 months before)

  • Deploy or update firewalls
  • Harden systems and configurations
  • Deploy malware protection across all systems
  • Implement patch management process
  • Establish access controls and authentication
  • Document all controls and configurations

Documentation Preparation (1 month before)

  • Create network diagrams and architecture documentation
  • Document security policies and procedures
  • Compile system inventory and configurations
  • Prepare evidence of control implementation
  • Organize IT staff and documentation
  • Plan on-site assessment logistics

Assessment Support (During assessment)

  • Provide IT staff to answer questions
  • Facilitate access to systems and facilities
  • Support technical testing activities
  • Provide documentation and evidence as needed
  • Address questions and clarifications

Common Issues in Cyber Essentials Plus Assessments

  • Firewall Gaps: Necessary services exposed, unnecessary ports open
  • Unpatched Systems: Systems missing critical security patches
  • Default Credentials: Unchanged default passwords on devices
  • Weak Access Controls: Users with excessive privileges
  • Malware Protection Gaps: Systems without protection or definitions out of date
  • Configuration Issues: Systems not properly hardened
  • Documentation Gaps: Policies and procedures not documented
  • Scope Confusion: Unclear what systems should be covered

Benefits of Cyber Essentials Plus

  • Independent third-party verification of security controls
  • High credibility and customer confidence
  • Competitive advantage in government procurement
  • Demonstrated commitment to information security
  • Identified vulnerabilities before they cause damage
  • Expert assessment of security posture
  • Recommendations for security improvement
  • Annual reassessment keeps controls current
  • Marketing advantage (can advertise certification)
  • Government supplier eligibility

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Plan and coordinate assessment process
  • Implement all five core security controls
  • Prepare documentation and evidence for assessment
  • Manage IT staff involvement in assessment
  • Address assessment findings and non-conformities
  • Remediate any vulnerabilities identified
  • Plan and execute annual reassessments
  • Maintain control effectiveness between assessments
  • Communicate assessment results to leadership
  • Update security practices based on assessment feedback

When to Choose Cyber Essentials Plus

Choose Cyber Essentials Plus if:

  • Bidding on government contracts requiring it
  • Customers require third-party verified security
  • Handling sensitive or critical data
  • Competing in high-security environments
  • Need to demonstrate security to stakeholders
  • Want expert assessment and recommendations
  • Budget allows for higher certification cost
  • Long-term security program investment

Standard Cyber Essentials may be sufficient if:

  • Government contracts not a major business driver
  • Customers accept self-assessed compliance
  • Budget constraints are significant
  • Starting initial security improvement
  • Planning to upgrade to Plus later

Cyber Essentials Plus and Continuous Improvement

Between Assessments

  • Maintain all controls in operational state
  • Continue patch management and updates
  • Monitor firewall and access controls
  • Verify malware protection is active
  • Review and improve procedures

Annual Reassessment Preparation

  • Review previous assessment findings
  • Verify all remediation has been maintained
  • Assess changes to IT environment
  • Update documentation as needed
  • Conduct internal assessment before external audit

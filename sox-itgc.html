SOX & ITGC

Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) compliance with focus on Information Technology General Controls (ITGC) – the framework ensuring the integrity of financial reporting systems

Overview

The Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) of 2002 is a U.S. federal law that mandates strict requirements for public companies to protect investors by improving the accuracy and reliability of corporate disclosures. Section 404 requires management to assess and report on the effectiveness of internal controls over financial reporting — a large part of which is **IT General Controls (ITGC)**.

What are ITGCs?

IT General Controls are the foundational controls that support the reliability of all automated financial systems. They typically fall into five key domains:

  • IT Governance & Management – Policies, roles, and oversight
  • Change Management – Controls over system changes and deployments
  • Access Management – User access provisioning, reviews, and least privilege
  • Operations & Security – Backup, recovery, job scheduling, and physical/logical security
  • System Development / Program Change – Controls over new system implementations and modifications

Why SOX & ITGC Matter for IT Directors

IT leaders are directly responsible for the design, implementation, and operating effectiveness of ITGCs. Weak ITGCs can lead to material weaknesses, qualified audit opinions, regulatory penalties, and loss of investor confidence. SOX compliance is a recurring annual process involving internal audit, external auditors, and significant documentation.

Common ITGC Control Areas

  • Segregation of Duties (SoD) in financial systems
  • User access reviews and privileged access management
  • Change management and approval workflows
  • System backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity testing
  • Logical and physical security of financial systems
  • IT risk assessment and monitoring

Relationship with Other Frameworks

SOX/ITGC is often mapped to COSO, COBIT, NIST, and ISO 27001. Many organizations use their existing ISO 27001 or SOC 2 controls to support SOX compliance, reducing duplication of effort.

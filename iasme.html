IASME

UK-based governance body and certification scheme for Cyber Essentials, Cyber Essentials Plus, and other information security standards targeted at SMEs and public sector suppliers

Overview

IASME (Information Assurance for Small and Medium Enterprises) is the UK government-appointed licensing body responsible for delivering the **Cyber Essentials** scheme. It provides certification, assessment, and governance for organizations seeking to demonstrate baseline cybersecurity controls.

Key Schemes Delivered by IASME

  • Cyber Essentials – Basic certification for fundamental technical controls
  • Cyber Essentials Plus – Enhanced certification with independent technical verification and on-site testing
  • IASME Governance Standard – Broader information security and data protection management system
  • Supply Chain Assurance – Assessments focused on third-party risk

Why IASME Matters in the UK (2026)

IASME certification is mandatory or highly preferred for many UK government contracts, local authorities, NHS suppliers, and organizations handling sensitive data. Cyber Essentials is often a prerequisite for bidding on public sector tenders and demonstrates due diligence to insurers and larger enterprise customers.

Key Differences: Cyber Essentials vs Plus

  • Cyber Essentials: Self-assessment + external validation of answers
  • Cyber Essentials Plus: Full technical verification including vulnerability scanning and simulated attacks

IT Director Considerations

IASME/Cyber Essentials requires ongoing maintenance of controls (firewalls, secure configuration, access control, malware protection, and patch management). Many organizations use IASME certification as a stepping stone toward ISO 27001 or as part of a broader supplier assurance program.

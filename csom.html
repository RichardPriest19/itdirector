CSOM

Certified Security Operations Manager – certification for professionals managing security operations centres and cyber defence functions

Overview

The Certified Security Operations Manager (CSOM) is a management-level certification for professionals overseeing Security Operations Centres (SOCs), cyber defence teams, and security monitoring functions. It addresses the leadership, process, and technical dimensions of running an effective SOC — covering team management, operational metrics, threat management governance, tooling strategy, and stakeholder reporting.

Key Value: CSOM bridges the gap between hands-on SOC analyst skills and security leadership — equipping security operations managers to build high-performing teams, optimise detection and response processes, and report security operational effectiveness to executive and board-level stakeholders.

Core Competency Areas

SOC Strategy & Governance

  • Define SOC mission, scope, service catalogue, and operational boundaries
  • Align SOC operations with the organisation’s security strategy, risk appetite, and regulatory requirements
  • Select and justify SOC operating models — in-house, outsourced, hybrid, and virtual SOC
  • Establish SOC governance, escalation paths, and executive reporting frameworks

Team Leadership & Development

  • Recruit, develop, and retain SOC analysts across all tier levels
  • Manage shift patterns, analyst wellbeing, and burnout in high-tempo SOC environments
  • Build career progression pathways and training plans for SOC team members
  • Foster a culture of continuous learning, threat knowledge sharing, and improvement

Operational Performance & Metrics

  • Define and track SOC KPIs — MTTD, MTTR, alert volume, escalation rates, and false positive ratios
  • Implement quality assurance processes for alert triage, incident handling, and documentation
  • Drive automation and SOAR integration to improve analyst efficiency and response speed
  • Benchmark SOC performance against industry standards and maturity models

Threat Management & Intelligence

  • Govern threat intelligence consumption and integration into SOC detection workflows
  • Oversee use case development, SIEM tuning, and detection coverage improvement
  • Manage relationships with threat intelligence providers, ISACs, and national CERTs
  • Lead major incident response coordination and executive communication during critical events

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Appoint CSOM-qualified professionals to lead SOC operations and cyber defence management
  • Use CSOM standards to evaluate SOC maturity, capability gaps, and investment priorities
  • Integrate SOC performance reporting into IT governance and executive risk reporting
  • Ensure the SOC manager has appropriate authority, budget, and access to senior stakeholders

Resources

  • SANS SOC Survey Reports – annual benchmarking of SOC maturity, tooling, and operational performance
  • MITRE ATT&CK – detection coverage framework central to SOC use case development and management
  • Gartner SOC Model Guide – operating model options and maturity benchmarks for SOC leadership
  • NCSC Cyber Security Operations Centre (CSOC) guidance – UK national guidance for SOC design and management
  • Exabeam State of the SOC Report – annual industry research on SOC metrics and challenges

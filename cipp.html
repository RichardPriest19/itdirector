CIPP

Certified Information Privacy Professional – IAPP certification in global privacy laws, regulations, and professional practice

Overview

The Certified Information Privacy Professional (CIPP) is the world’s most recognised privacy certification, awarded by the International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP). It validates knowledge of privacy laws, regulations, and enforcement across multiple jurisdictions. CIPP is available in regional specialisations — CIPP/E (Europe/GDPR), CIPP/US (United States), CIPP/A (Asia), and CIPP/C (Canada) — allowing professionals to demonstrate jurisdiction-specific expertise.

Key Value: CIPP is the global benchmark for privacy law knowledge — recognised by regulators, law firms, technology companies, and government agencies as demonstrating authoritative competency in data protection and privacy legislation.

CIPP Specialisations

CIPP/E – Europe (GDPR)

  • GDPR principles, legal bases, data subject rights, and controller/processor obligations
  • National implementing laws and supervisory authority enforcement across EU member states
  • UK GDPR, adequacy decisions, and cross-border transfer mechanisms
  • DPIAs, records of processing, privacy notices, and consent management

CIPP/US – United States

  • Federal privacy laws — HIPAA, COPPA, FERPA, GLBA, and sector-specific regulations
  • State privacy legislation — CCPA, CPRA, Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, and others
  • FTC enforcement, state AG actions, and private rights of action
  • US data breach notification laws across all 50 states

CIPP/A & CIPP/C

  • CIPP/A covers data protection laws across Asia-Pacific including APPI (Japan), PDPA (Singapore/Thailand), and PIPL (China)
  • CIPP/C covers Canadian privacy law including PIPEDA, Bill C-27 (CPPA), and provincial legislation

Exam Details

  • 90 multiple-choice questions over 2.5 hours per specialisation
  • No formal prerequisites — open to any professional working in or entering privacy practice
  • Renewal: 20 CPE credits annually with active IAPP membership
  • Many professionals hold multiple CIPP specialisations alongside CIPM and/or CIPT

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Require CIPP/E (or relevant regional variant) for all DPO, privacy officer, and senior data governance appointments
  • Use CIPP knowledge as a baseline for IT staff handling personal data in system design and procurement
  • Ensure CIPP-qualified staff lead privacy impact assessments and regulatory compliance reviews
  • Support staff in maintaining CPE credits to keep CIPP credentials current with evolving legislation

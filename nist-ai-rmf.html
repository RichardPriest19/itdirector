NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

Voluntary U.S. framework for identifying, measuring, and managing risks of AI systems across the lifecycle

Core Functions

  • Govern – Culture, policies, and accountability
  • Map – Context and risk identification
  • Measure – Assessment and tracking of risks
  • Manage – Prioritization and mitigation

Benefits for IT Leaders

Provides a structured, repeatable process to embed trustworthiness into AI initiatives. Complements ISO 42001 and helps demonstrate due diligence for regulators and boards.

Related: EU AI Act

The EU AI Act (phased implementation through 2026–2027) uses a risk-based approach (prohibited, high-risk, limited, minimal). High-risk systems require conformity assessments, transparency, and human oversight. Many organizations align NIST AI RMF practices with EU AI Act obligations.

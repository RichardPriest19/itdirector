CISM

Certified Information Security Manager – premier certification for security management & leadership

Overview

CISM (Certified Information Security Manager), administered by ISACA, is the leading certification for professionals focused on designing, managing, and assessing enterprise information security programs. The current exam content outline (last major refresh 2022, still in force in March 2026) covers four domains: Information Security Governance, Information Risk Management, Information Security Program Development and Management, and Information Security Incident Management. In 2026, CISM places strong emphasis on **AI security governance**, **cyber resilience**, **third-party risk**, **privacy integration**, **zero trust**, **regulatory compliance** (EU AI Act, NIS2, DORA, GDPR, CCPA), and **business-aligned security**. It requires 5 years of information security work experience (3 in management roles), adherence to the ISACA Code of Professional Ethics, and 120 CPE credits every 3 years. CISM is highly valued for CISO, security manager, risk officer, consultant, and governance roles in enterprise, government, finance, healthcare, and regulated industries.

Key Value: CISM validates strategic, management-level expertise in aligning security programs with business objectives — focusing on governance, risk management, program development, and incident response — making it the premier credential for security leadership roles, board-level credibility, and demonstrating due diligence in regulated environments.

Core Domains (2022–2026 Exam Outline)

1. Information Security Governance (17%)

  • Security strategy & alignment with business objectives
  • Governance frameworks & roles/responsibilities
  • Regulatory compliance & policy development

2. Information Risk Management (20%)

  • Risk assessment & treatment methodologies
  • Third-party risk & supply chain security
  • Risk monitoring & reporting

3. Information Security Program Development and Management (33%)

  • Program design & resource allocation
  • Security controls selection & implementation
  • Security awareness & training programs

4. Information Security Incident Management (30%)

  • Incident response planning & execution
  • Business continuity & disaster recovery integration
  • Post-incident review & lessons learned

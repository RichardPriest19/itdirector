CISM

Certified Information Security Manager – ISACA certification for information security management and governance professionals

Overview

The Certified Information Security Manager (CISM) is awarded by ISACA and is designed for professionals who manage, design, oversee, and assess enterprise information security programmes. It focuses on security management and governance rather than technical implementation, making it ideal for CISOs, security managers, and IT Directors with security oversight responsibilities.

Key Value: CISM is the leading management-focused security certification globally — demonstrating that the holder can govern, manage, and align information security with business objectives, and is held by over 50,000 professionals worldwide.

Core Exam Domains

Information Security Governance

  • Establish and maintain an information security governance framework aligned to business strategy
  • Define security policies, standards, and procedures consistent with organisational risk appetite
  • Align security governance with COBIT, ISO/IEC 27001, and NIST CSF
  • Report security programme performance and risk posture to executive management and the board

Information Risk Management

  • Identify, assess, and prioritise information security risks using recognised frameworks
  • Implement risk treatment strategies — accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid
  • Integrate risk management into IT projects, change management, and third-party procurement
  • Maintain a risk register and report risk status to senior stakeholders

Information Security Programme Development & Management

  • Design and implement an information security programme aligned to business and regulatory requirements
  • Manage security budgets, resource allocation, and security technology investments
  • Build a security-aware culture through training, communication, and behavioural programmes
  • Govern third-party security obligations through contracts, SLAs, and ongoing assessments

Incident Management

  • Establish and maintain an incident response and management capability
  • Define escalation procedures, communication plans, and stakeholder notification workflows
  • Lead post-incident reviews to identify lessons learned and drive programme improvement

Exam Details

  • 150 multiple-choice questions over 4 hours
  • Passing score: 450 out of 800 (scaled)
  • Prerequisite: 5 years of information security management experience (substitutions allowed)
  • Renewal: 120 CPE credits over 3 years

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Hold or require CISM for CISO, Head of Security, and senior security management appointments
  • Use CISM as the governance-level complement to technical certifications such as CISSP
  • Ensure security programme design, risk reporting, and incident management align with CISM standards
  • Integrate CISM-governed security management into wider IT governance and board reporting

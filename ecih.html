ECIH

EC-Council Certified Incident Handler – EC-Council certification for cybersecurity incident response and crisis management

Overview

The EC-Council Certified Incident Handler (ECIH) is awarded by EC-Council and provides a structured framework for professionals who manage cybersecurity incidents from initial detection through containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident review. ECIH covers incident response across multiple threat domains including malware, network attacks, insider threats, cloud incidents, and OT/ICS environments.

Key Value: ECIH equips incident response professionals with a comprehensive, repeatable methodology for handling security incidents — reducing the time to containment, limiting business impact, and ensuring defensible, audit-ready documentation throughout the response lifecycle.

Core Exam Domains

Incident Handling & Response Fundamentals

  • Apply the EC-Council incident handling framework — preparation, detection, containment, eradication, and recovery
  • Establish an Incident Response Team (IRT) with defined roles, authority, and communication protocols
  • Develop and maintain incident response playbooks for diverse threat scenarios
  • Apply legal, regulatory, and evidence handling requirements to incident response activities

Threat-Specific Incident Handling

  • Handle malware incidents — ransomware, RATs, rootkits, and fileless malware
  • Respond to network-based attacks — DDoS, man-in-the-middle, and lateral movement
  • Manage insider threat incidents with appropriate confidentiality and HR coordination
  • Handle cloud security incidents across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud environments

Forensic Evidence & Investigation

  • Collect and preserve volatile and non-volatile digital evidence during incident response
  • Perform memory forensics, disk imaging, and log analysis in support of incident investigation
  • Maintain chain of custody and produce investigation documentation suitable for legal proceedings

Post-Incident Activity

  • Conduct post-incident reviews to identify root cause and contributing factors
  • Produce incident reports for management, regulators, and affected stakeholders
  • Implement lessons-learned improvements to detection, response, and prevention capabilities

Exam Details

  • Exam code: 212-89 — 100 multiple-choice questions over 3 hours
  • Recommended prerequisite: 1 year of cybersecurity or IT operations experience
  • Renewal: 120 EC-Council Continuing Education (ECE) credits every 3 years

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Require ECIH for security operations staff with incident response responsibilities
  • Use ECIH as the structured foundation for the organisation’s incident response programme and playbook development
  • Ensure incident response procedures are tested against ECIH standards through tabletop exercises and simulations
  • Coordinate ECIH-qualified responders with legal, communications, and executive teams during major incidents

NIS / NIS2

EU Network and Information Systems Directive – cybersecurity requirements for operators of essential services & important entities

Overview

The NIS Directive (NIS1 – 2016/1148) was the first EU-wide cybersecurity law for critical infrastructure. In March 2026, it has been fully replaced by **NIS2 Directive (EU 2022/2555)**, which entered into force in 2023, with transposition deadline October 2024 and active enforcement across all EU Member States. NIS2 significantly expands scope (adds important entities, supply-chain obligations), strengthens risk management requirements, mandates incident reporting (24h initial, 72h update, 1 month final report), introduces board-level accountability, higher fines (up to €10M or 2% global turnover), and harmonizes supervision. It covers 18 critical sectors + digital infrastructure and applies to medium/large organizations in those sectors, as well as many smaller entities if deemed critical. NIS2 aligns with DORA (financial sector), CRA (cyber resilience), CSA (cybersecurity certification), and GDPR.

Key Value: NIS2 compliance reduces systemic cyber risk, protects critical services & supply chains, avoids severe fines & reputational damage, drives board-level security ownership, and — when combined with Zero Trust & ISO 27001 — builds a robust, defensible cybersecurity posture for EU operations.

Core Requirements of NIS2 (2026)

Scope & Entity Classification

  • Essential entities (energy, transport, banking, health, water, digital infra, space)
  • Important entities (manufacturing, postal, waste management, food, research, digital providers)
  • Medium & large organizations in scope; smaller ones if critical

Risk Management & Security Measures

  • All-hazards approach & proportionate technical/organizational measures
  • Supply-chain security & third-party risk management
  • Business continuity, crisis management, backup & recovery

Incident Reporting & Notification

  • 24-hour early warning, 72-hour detailed report, 1-month final report
  • Significant incidents affecting service availability or data security
  • Voluntary early reporting encouraged

Governance & Liability

  • Management body responsibility & training obligation
  • Administrative fines up to €10M or 2% global turnover
  • Supervisory & enforcement powers (on-site inspections, audits)

Key Enforcement & Trends in 2026

National Implementation

  • All EU Member States have transposed NIS2 into national law
  • Competent authorities & CSIRTs fully operational
  • Cross-border cooperation via Cooperation Group & CSIRTs Network

Supply Chain & Third-Party Focus

  • Mandatory supply-chain risk assessments
  • Contractual obligations on providers of critical services
  • Increased scrutiny of cloud, managed security & IT vendors

Alignment & Overlap

  • Strong links with DORA (finance), CRA (products), CSA (certification)
  • Harmonization with GDPR breach notification & Zero Trust practices
  • Increasing overlap with NIS2 reporting & ISO 27001 controls

Best Practices for NIS2 Compliance (2026)

Risk & Security

  • Conduct NIS2-scope & entity classification assessment
  • Implement all-hazards risk management & supply-chain controls
  • Adopt Zero Trust & strong authentication (MFA, phishing-resistant)

Incident Management

  • Establish 24/72h/1m reporting process & playbook
  • Test incident response & notification procedures
  • Integrate with national CSIRT & sector-specific reporting

Governance & Assurance

  • Assign management body accountability & training
  • Conduct regular NIS2 audits & gap analyses
  • Maintain documentation & evidence for competent authorities

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Determine NIS2 applicability & entity classification
  • Approve risk management framework & supply-chain strategy
  • Ensure board-level accountability & cybersecurity training
  • Oversee incident reporting readiness & 24/72h process
  • Monitor national enforcement, fines & cross-border cooperation
  • Drive Zero Trust, MFA & encryption adoption
  • Align NIS2 with DORA, GDPR, ISO 27001 & existing controls

CASP+

CompTIA Advanced Security Practitioner – advanced-level cybersecurity certification for enterprise security architecture and risk management

Overview

CASP+ (CompTIA Advanced Security Practitioner) is an advanced-level, vendor-neutral cybersecurity certification awarded by CompTIA. It is designed for experienced security professionals who implement security solutions rather than purely managing or planning them. Unlike many management-focused certifications, CASP+ is a practitioner-level qualification that validates technical skills across enterprise security architecture, risk management, cryptography, and integration of enterprise security.

Key Value: CASP+ is the only hands-on, performance-based certification for practitioners — not managers — at the advanced skill level. It is DoD 8570/8140-approved and widely recognised as demonstrating mastery of enterprise security engineering and operations.

Core Exam Domains (CAS-004 / 2026)

Security Architecture

  • Design and implement secure enterprise network architectures
  • Apply Zero Trust principles, microsegmentation, and software-defined networking (SDN)
  • Integrate cloud, hybrid, and on-premises environments securely
  • Evaluate cryptographic solutions and key management strategies

Security Operations

  • Perform threat hunting, analysis, and advanced incident response
  • Leverage SIEM, SOAR, and EDR platforms for continuous monitoring
  • Conduct vulnerability assessments and penetration testing coordination
  • Analyse indicators of compromise (IOCs) and threat intelligence feeds

Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC)

  • Apply risk management frameworks (NIST RMF, ISO 31000) at enterprise scale
  • Align security controls with regulatory requirements (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS)
  • Develop and assess privacy and data protection policies
  • Manage third-party risk and vendor security assessments

Security Engineering & Cryptography

  • Implement PKI, certificate management, and advanced cryptographic protocols
  • Secure industrial control systems (ICS/SCADA) and IoT environments
  • Apply secure software development practices and DevSecOps principles
  • Harden operating systems, containers, and virtualisation platforms

Exam Details

Format & Requirements

  • Exam code: CAS-004 (current version as of 2026)
  • Up to 90 questions — a mix of multiple-choice and performance-based questions (PBQs)
  • 180-minute exam duration
  • No passing score published — CompTIA uses a scaled scoring system (pass/fail)
  • Recommended prerequisite: 10 years of IT experience, including at least 5 years of hands-on security experience

Renewal & Continuing Education

  • Certification valid for 3 years from the date of passing
  • Renew via CompTIA's Continuing Education (CE) programme — 75 CEUs required over 3 years
  • CEUs earned through training, conferences, publications, or retaking the exam
  • Passing a higher-level CompTIA certification (e.g., CySA+, PenTest+) also renews CASP+

Key Trends & Best Practices in 2026

Zero Trust & Identity-Centric Security

  • Implement Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) across enterprise environments
  • Integrate continuous authentication and adaptive access controls
  • Apply microsegmentation to limit lateral movement and blast radius

Cloud & Hybrid Security

  • Secure multi-cloud and hybrid deployments using shared responsibility models
  • Implement Cloud Security Posture Management (CSPM) and SaaS security controls
  • Apply container and Kubernetes security hardening practices

AI-Augmented Security Operations

  • Leverage AI/ML-powered threat detection and automated response (SOAR)
  • Apply adversarial AI awareness — defending against AI-assisted attacks
  • Integrate threat intelligence platforms for proactive defence

Supply Chain & Third-Party Risk

  • Conduct rigorous vendor risk assessments and supply chain security reviews
  • Implement Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) practices for software supply chain visibility
  • Apply NIST SP 800-161 and related frameworks for supply chain risk management

CASP+ vs. Related Certifications

CASP+ vs. CISSP

  • CISSP (ISC²) is management and policy-oriented; CASP+ is technical and hands-on
  • CISSP suits CISOs and security managers; CASP+ suits security architects and senior engineers
  • Both are DoD 8570/8140-approved at the IAT/IAM Level III category

CASP+ vs. CISM

  • CISM (ISACA) focuses on security governance and programme management
  • CASP+ is more technical, covering implementation, architecture, and engineering depth
  • Professionals may hold both to demonstrate both strategic and technical credibility

CASP+ vs. CEH / OSCP

  • CEH and OSCP focus predominantly on offensive security and penetration testing
  • CASP+ provides a broader enterprise security architecture and defence focus
  • For red team specialists, OSCP/CEH complement CASP+ rather than replace it

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Encourage senior security engineers and architects to pursue CASP+ as a benchmark of advanced competency
  • Use CASP+ as a hiring and promotion criterion for principal-level security roles
  • Incorporate CASP+ into skills frameworks for DoD-aligned or government contract environments
  • Support continuing education and CEU attainment to maintain certification currency
  • Align CASP+ holders with enterprise security architecture, red/blue team leadership, and GRC functions

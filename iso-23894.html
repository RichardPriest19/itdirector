ISO/IEC 23894

ISO/IEC 23894 – guidance on risk management for artificial intelligence systems (2023 edition)

Overview

ISO/IEC 23894:2023 is the first international standard providing guidance on identifying, analyzing, evaluating, treating, and monitoring risks associated with artificial intelligence (AI) systems throughout their lifecycle. It is designed to complement ISO/IEC 42001 (AIMS) by offering practical, risk-based approaches to managing AI-specific risks (technical, ethical, societal, operational, legal). In March 2026, ISO/IEC 23894 is the globally recognized reference for AI risk management, increasingly used for EU AI Act compliance (high-risk & systemic risk obligations), enterprise AI governance, risk registers, third-party AI provider assessments, investor due diligence, and alignment with NIST AI RMF, OECD AI Principles, and privacy/ethics frameworks (GDPR, ISO 27701). It emphasizes context-aware risk assessment, stakeholder involvement, transparency, fairness, robustness, accountability, and continual monitoring.

Key Value: ISO/IEC 23894 provides structured, internationally agreed guidance for managing AI risks — enabling organizations to identify and mitigate ethical, legal, technical, and societal risks, demonstrate responsible AI practices, comply with emerging regulations (EU AI Act), build stakeholder trust, and scale AI safely and sustainably.

Core Risk Management Guidance (2023 Edition – 2026)

Risk Management Process

  • Context establishment & AI system scope
  • Risk identification (technical, ethical, societal, operational)
  • Risk analysis & evaluation (likelihood, severity, impact)
  • Risk treatment (mitigation, acceptance, transfer)
  • Risk monitoring, review & continual improvement

Key AI Risk Categories

  • Technical risks (robustness, reliability, security, bias)
  • Ethical & societal risks (fairness, transparency, accountability)
  • Legal & compliance risks (data protection, liability, regulation)
  • Operational & business risks (supply chain, third-party, sustainability)

Integration & Alignment

  • Companion to ISO/IEC 42001 (AIMS)
  • Supports EU AI Act risk classification & obligations
  • Aligns with NIST AI RMF, OECD AI Principles, ISO 31000

Key Adoption & Trends in 2026

Regulatory & Compliance

  • Strong mapping to EU AI Act high-risk & systemic risk requirements
  • Referenced in public procurement & investor due diligence
  • Used for AI risk registers & third-party assessments

Responsible AI Governance

  • Focus on bias mitigation, transparency, explainability
  • Privacy & data governance in AI (link to ISO 27701)
  • Sustainability & environmental impact of AI systems

Emerging AI Risks

  • Guidance for generative AI & large language models
  • Supply chain & third-party AI provider risks
  • Integration with Zero Trust & secure AI development

Best Practices for ISO/IEC 23894 Adoption (2026)

Risk Assessment

  • Conduct AI-specific risk & impact assessments
  • Involve stakeholders & diverse teams
  • Document risks, likelihood, severity & treatment plans

Risk Treatment & Controls

  • Prioritize mitigation for high-impact risks (bias, security)
  • Implement controls from ISO 42001 Annex A
  • Establish monitoring & review mechanisms

Continual Improvement

  • Integrate AI risk into enterprise risk management
  • Update for emerging risks (gen AI, multimodal)
  • Conduct regular risk reviews & audits

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion ISO/IEC 23894 as core AI risk management reference
  • Approve AI risk register & treatment plans
  • Ensure AI impact & risk assessments are conducted
  • Oversee ethical guidelines, transparency & accountability
  • Monitor compliance mapping (EU AI Act, GDPR, etc.)
  • Drive AI risk integration with ISO 27001 & Zero Trust
  • Plan for generative AI & supply chain risk controls

CAN-SPAM Act

U.S. federal law regulating commercial email messages – the primary legal framework governing bulk email, marketing campaigns, and transactional messages in the United States

Overview

The CAN-SPAM Act (Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography And Marketing Act of 2003) establishes national standards for sending commercial email. It applies to all commercial electronic mail messages whose primary purpose is the advertisement or promotion of a commercial product or service.

Core Requirements

  • No false or misleading header information
  • Accurate subject lines that reflect the content of the message
  • Clear identification that the message is an advertisement
  • Valid physical postal address of the sender
  • Conspicuous and functional unsubscribe / opt-out mechanism
  • Honoring opt-out requests within 10 business days

Key Provisions (2026)

  • Applies to both B2C and B2B commercial emails
  • Transactional and relationship messages are exempt if they meet specific criteria
  • Violations can result in fines up to $51,744 per email (adjusted for inflation)
  • Aggravated violations (harvesting addresses, using open relays, etc.) carry higher penalties
  • Enforced by the FTC, with possible state attorney general actions

IT Director & Compliance Responsibilities

IT and marketing teams must ensure:

  • Proper email infrastructure and header configuration
  • Functional unsubscribe processes and suppression list management
  • Accurate sender identification and physical address inclusion
  • Integration between marketing platforms (Mailchimp, Campaign Monitor, etc.) and compliance systems
  • Regular audits of email lists and consent records

Relation to Other Regulations

CAN-SPAM is often used alongside GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and PECR. While CAN-SPAM focuses on “opt-out”, many organizations adopt an “opt-in” approach to align with stricter international privacy laws.

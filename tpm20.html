TPM 2.0

Trusted Platform Module 2.0 – hardware root of trust for secure boot, encryption & device identity

Overview

TPM 2.0 (Trusted Platform Module 2.0) is the current industry-standard hardware-based security module (specified in ISO/IEC 11889:2015 and TCG TPM 2.0 Library) that provides a hardware root of trust for devices. Mandatory for Windows 11 (and strongly recommended for Windows 10/11 Pro/Enterprise), TPM 2.0 enables **secure boot**, **measured boot**, **BitLocker full-volume encryption**, **Windows Hello for Business**, **Credential Guard**, **Device Guard**, **attestation**, **secure key storage**, **cryptographic operations** (RSA, ECC, SHA-256, AES), and **device identity** (endorsement key). In March 2026 (Windows 11 24H2 / 25H2 era), TPM 2.0 is deeply integrated with **Microsoft Pluton** (firmware TPM in newer silicon), **Microsoft Entra ID**, **Intune**, **Microsoft Defender for Endpoint**, and **Zero Trust** architecture. It supports **firmware TPM** (fTPM in AMD/Intel CPUs), **discrete TPM** chips, and **virtual TPM** in VMs, with strong compliance alignment (FIPS 140-2/3, NIST 800-193, ISO/IEC 19790, GDPR, HIPAA).

Key Value: TPM 2.0 delivers hardware-enforced root of trust, tamper-resistant key storage, secure boot, and device attestation — enabling strong data protection (BitLocker), password-less authentication (Windows Hello), credential isolation (Credential Guard), and Zero Trust device health verification with minimal performance impact and high assurance.

Core Capabilities (2026)

Root of Trust & Secure Boot

  • Measured boot & PCR (Platform Configuration Registers)
  • Secure Boot chain-of-trust validation
  • Attestation & remote device health proof

Cryptography & Key Management

  • RSA/ECC key generation & storage
  • AES encryption/decryption & SHA-256 hashing
  • Endorsement key (EK) & storage root key (SRK)

Integration & Features

  • BitLocker automatic enablement & recovery key escrow
  • Windows Hello for Business & FIDO2 support
  • Credential Guard & Device Guard (Virtualization-based Security)

Compliance & Security

  • FIPS 140-2/3 Level 2+ certification
  • ISO/IEC 19790 & TCG TPM 2.0 compliance
  • Zero Trust device health & attestation

Key Features & Trends in 2026

Pluton & Firmware TPM

  • Microsoft Pluton (fTPM in AMD/Intel/Qualcomm silicon)
  • Enhanced tamper resistance & secure firmware updates
  • Pluton integration with Windows Hello & Defender

Zero Trust & Remote Attestation

  • Device health attestation to Entra ID & Intune
  • Conditional Access based on TPM presence & PCR values
  • Integration with Microsoft Defender for Endpoint

Compliance & Enterprise

  • Mandatory for Windows 11 compliance & security baselines
  • Strong alignment with NIST 800-193, FedRAMP, ISO 27001
  • Support for ARM64 & Windows on ARM devices

Best Practices for TPM 2.0 Adoption (2026)

Deployment & Policy

  • Require TPM 2.0 in device procurement & Intune policies
  • Enable BitLocker & recovery key escrow to Entra ID
  • Enforce Secure Boot & measured boot

Security & Governance

  • Integrate TPM attestation with Conditional Access
  • Use Credential Guard & Virtualization-based Security
  • Monitor TPM health & PCR values via Defender

Compliance & Assurance

  • Align with NIST 800-193 & ISO 27001 controls
  • Conduct regular TPM & device health audits
  • Plan for Pluton-enabled devices & firmware updates

  • Align TPM with ISO 27001, NIST & regulatory needs

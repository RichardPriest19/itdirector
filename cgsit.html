CGSIT

Certified in Governance, Security, and Information Technology – integrated certification covering IT governance, security management, and technology risk

Overview

The Certified in Governance, Security, and Information Technology (CGSIT) is an integrated professional certification for IT leaders and senior practitioners who need to demonstrate competency across the interconnected domains of IT governance, information security management, and technology risk. It is suited to professionals bridging technical and strategic roles in organisations where governance and security cannot be treated in isolation.

Key Value: CGSIT provides a unified credential for professionals operating at the intersection of IT governance, risk, and security — making it particularly relevant for IT Directors, GRC leads, and senior security managers who must align technical controls with business strategy and regulatory requirements.

Core Competency Areas

IT Governance Frameworks

  • Apply COBIT, ITIL, and ISO/IEC 38500 to align IT strategy with organisational objectives
  • Establish governance structures, board-level reporting, and IT decision-making frameworks
  • Define IT policies, standards, and procedures that support organisational risk appetite
  • Implement IT performance measurement using KPIs, KRIs, and maturity models

Security Management

  • Align information security management with ISO/IEC 27001 and NIST CSF
  • Govern security operations, threat management, and incident response at enterprise scale
  • Manage third-party and supply chain security risk within the governance framework
  • Oversee identity and access management, data protection, and privacy compliance

Technology Risk & Compliance

  • Conduct IT risk assessments aligned to NIST RMF, ISO 31000, and COBIT
  • Maintain compliance with sector-specific regulations and reporting obligations
  • Integrate risk management into IT project delivery and change management processes
  • Report risk posture and compliance status to executive and board-level stakeholders

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Position CGSIT as a benchmark qualification for senior IT governance, risk, and compliance roles
  • Use CGSIT-qualified professionals to lead GRC programmes, board reporting, and IT audit liaison
  • Align CGSIT competency areas with the organisation’s IT governance and risk management frameworks
  • Support CGSIT study and examination as part of the IT leadership development pathway

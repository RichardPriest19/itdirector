CDE

Certified Digital Examiner – professional certification for digital forensics examination, evidence handling, and cyber investigation

Overview

The Certified Digital Examiner (CDE) is a practitioner-level certification for professionals conducting digital forensic examinations, cybercrime investigations, and electronic evidence analysis. It validates competency in acquiring, preserving, analysing, and presenting digital evidence across storage media, operating systems, networks, and cloud environments in a legally defensible manner.

Key Value: CDE demonstrates hands-on competency in the full digital evidence lifecycle — from forensically sound acquisition through court-ready reporting — supporting both law enforcement and corporate investigation functions.

Core Competency Areas

Evidence Acquisition & Preservation

  • Perform forensically sound imaging of hard drives, SSDs, USB media, and mobile devices
  • Apply write-blocking hardware and software to maintain evidence integrity
  • Manage chain of custody documentation from scene through examination to presentation
  • Conduct live acquisition and volatile memory capture for time-sensitive investigations

File System & Artefact Analysis

  • Examine NTFS, FAT, exFAT, ext4, and APFS file system structures
  • Recover deleted files, unallocated space data, and carved file fragments
  • Interpret file metadata, timestamps, access logs, and system artefacts
  • Analyse Windows Registry, event logs, prefetch, and shellbag artefacts

Network & Cloud Forensics

  • Capture and analyse network traffic, log files, and intrusion artefacts
  • Acquire evidence from cloud platforms including Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace
  • Examine email headers, server logs, and communication metadata
  • Apply legal process and API-based evidence collection for cloud-hosted data

Reporting & Legal Presentation

  • Produce clear, court-admissible forensic examination reports with hash verification
  • Present findings as an expert witness in civil, criminal, and employment proceedings
  • Apply jurisdiction-specific rules on digital evidence admissibility

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Maintain CDE-qualified personnel within the IT security or incident response function
  • Ensure forensic procedures comply with ACPO Good Practice Guide and ISO/IEC 27037
  • Retain forensic tools, write-blockers, and secure evidence storage as part of operational readiness
  • Coordinate with legal and HR teams to ensure investigations follow defensible, documented processes

Resources

  • ACPO Good Practice Guide for Digital Evidence – foundational UK forensic evidence handling guidance
  • NIST SP 800-86 – Guide to Integrating Forensic Techniques into Incident Response
  • ISO/IEC 27037 – international standard for digital evidence identification, collection, and preservation
  • Autopsy, FTK, EnCase – leading forensic tools covered in CDE examination preparation
  • SANS FOR500 / FOR508 – complementary advanced forensic training aligned with CDE competency areas

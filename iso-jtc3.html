IEC/ISO JTC 3 – Joint Technical Committee on Quantum Technologies standardization

Overview

IEC/ISO JTC 3 (Joint Technical Committee 3: Quantum technologies) was established in 2024 by the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) and International Organization for Standardization (ISO) to develop international standards in quantum technologies. As of March 2026, JTC 3 is fully operational, with a growing membership (27+ participating countries, 11+ observers), multiple active working groups (e.g., quantum computing supply chain, quantum key distribution, quantum-safe cryptography), plenary meetings (e.g., Seoul 2024 inaugural, Copenhagen Nov 2025), and coordination with sector-specific committees (e.g., IEC/TC 47 semiconductors, ISO/TC 22 road vehicles, ETSI quantum groups). Its scope covers quantum information technologies (computing, simulation, communication, sensing, metrology), quantum-safe cryptography, hardware/software ecosystems, and applications across science, industry, and society. It aims to set the foundation for quantum technologies to reach full potential while ensuring consistency, interoperability, and global alignment.

Key Value: IEC/ISO JTC 3 provides the global platform for coordinated quantum standards development — enabling interoperability, supply chain trust, regulatory alignment (EU AI Act, NIS2), risk reduction, and accelerated safe adoption of quantum technologies across computing, sensing, communication, and cryptography.

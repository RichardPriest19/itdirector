SOC 2

Service Organization Control 2 – the leading assurance report for service providers demonstrating controls relevant to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy

Overview

SOC 2 is an attestation report developed by the American Institute of CPAs (AICPA). It evaluates a service organization’s controls based on the Trust Services Criteria (TSC). Unlike SOC 1 (which focuses on financial reporting), SOC 2 is designed for technology and cloud service providers that handle customer data.

Trust Services Criteria (TSC)

  • Security – The foundational criterion (required for all SOC 2 reports)
  • Availability – System is available for operation and use as committed
  • Processing Integrity – System processing is complete, valid, accurate, timely, and authorized
  • Confidentiality – Information designated as confidential is protected
  • Privacy – Personal information is collected, used, retained, disclosed, and disposed of appropriately

Types of SOC 2 Reports

  • Type 1 – Evaluates the design of controls at a specific point in time
  • Type 2 – Evaluates both the design and operating effectiveness of controls over a period of time (usually 6–12 months) – most commonly requested

Why SOC 2 Matters in 2026

SOC 2 has become the de facto standard for technology vendors, SaaS providers, and service organizations. Customers, auditors, and regulators frequently require a current SOC 2 Type 2 report as part of vendor due diligence, contract negotiations, and insurance requirements.

IT Director / CISO Responsibilities

  • Implementing and maintaining controls across the five Trust Services Criteria
  • Working with external auditors for annual SOC 2 examinations
  • Mapping existing frameworks (ISO 27001, NIST, CIS Controls) to SOC 2 requirements
  • Managing the bridge letter process for customers needing interim assurance
  • Ensuring continuous monitoring and evidence collection throughout the year

Relation to Other Frameworks

SOC 2 often complements ISO 27001, PCI DSS, HIPAA, and GDPR. Many organizations pursue both SOC 2 and ISO 27001 to satisfy a broad range of customer and regulatory demands.

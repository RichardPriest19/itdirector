RAID Logs

Project governance tool that centrally tracks Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies throughout the project lifecycle

Overview

RAID Logs are a fundamental project management artifact used to maintain visibility and control over the four key elements that can impact project success. They serve as a living document reviewed regularly in project meetings, steering committees, and executive updates.

RAID Breakdown

  • Risks – Potential future events that may positively or negatively affect the project (with probability, impact, owner, mitigation, and contingency plans)
  • Assumptions – Factors believed to be true but not yet verified (with validation owner and review date)
  • Issues – Current problems or blockers already impacting the project (with severity, owner, due date, and resolution status)
  • Dependencies – External deliverables, decisions, or resources the project relies upon (with owner and expected delivery date)

Why RAID Logs Are Essential

In complex IT projects, digital transformations, infrastructure upgrades, and vendor implementations, RAID Logs provide a single source of truth for governance. They help prevent surprises, support informed decision-making, and demonstrate effective project oversight to stakeholders and auditors.

Best Practices in 2026

  • Maintain a single, live RAID Log accessible to all stakeholders
  • Use consistent scoring (e.g., RAG status: Red/Amber/Green)
  • Assign clear owners and realistic due dates for every item
  • Review and update the log in every project meeting
  • Integrate with project tools such as Microsoft Project, Jira, ServiceNow, or SharePoint
  • Escalate high-impact risks and critical issues to senior leadership promptly

IT Director Role

As an IT leader, you are expected to ensure RAID Logs are actively maintained for all major initiatives, used as input for risk committees, and linked to broader enterprise risk management processes.

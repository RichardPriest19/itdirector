RAID Logs

Project management tool that tracks Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies in a single, structured register

Overview

RAID Logs are a fundamental project governance tool used by project managers, programme managers, and IT directors to maintain visibility and control over the key factors that can impact project success. The acronym stands for **Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies**.

Components of a RAID Log

  • Risks – Potential future events that could affect the project (with probability, impact, owner, and mitigation actions)
  • Assumptions – Statements believed to be true but not yet proven (with validation plan and owner)
  • Issues – Current problems or obstacles that are already impacting the project (with severity, owner, and resolution plan)
  • Dependencies – External factors or deliverables the project relies on (with owner and due date)

Why RAID Logs Are Critical for IT Directors

In complex IT programmes, digital transformations, and infrastructure projects, RAID Logs provide a single source of truth for governance, risk oversight, and decision-making. They are commonly reviewed in Project Board, Steering Committee, and Executive meetings.

Best Practice in 2026

  • Maintain a live, centrally accessible RAID Log (often in Microsoft Project, ServiceNow, Jira, or SharePoint)
  • Assign clear owners and due dates for every item
  • Regularly review and update during project meetings
  • Link risks and issues to the project risk register and issue management process
  • Use RAG (Red/Amber/Green) status for quick visibility

Tools Commonly Used

Microsoft Project, Excel templates, ServiceNow Project Portfolio Management, Jira Align, Monday.com, and custom Power BI dashboards.

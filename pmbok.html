PMBOK

Project Management Body of Knowledge – PMI’s global standard for project management principles & practices

Overview

The **PMBOK® Guide – Seventh Edition** (2021) is the current authoritative edition of the Project Management Body of Knowledge, published by PMI. It shifts from a process-based approach (Sixth Edition) to a principles-based framework, focusing on **12 universal principles** and **8 performance domains**, with strong emphasis on tailoring, value delivery, stakeholder engagement, systems thinking, and hybrid project lifecycles. In March 2026, PMBOK Seventh Edition remains the global reference standard, forming the basis for the PMP® exam (50%+ agile/hybrid content), PMO maturity models, and organizational project governance. PMIstandards+™ provides digital, living extensions (models, artifacts, practice guides) that complement the Guide. It aligns with Agile, DevOps, OKRs, value stream management, and emerging AI-assisted project practices.

Key Value: PMBOK provides a universal, flexible framework that improves project success rates, standardizes terminology & practices, supports tailoring for predictive/agile/hybrid environments, and enables organizations to deliver consistent value while adapting to change and complexity.

Core Structure (Seventh Edition – 2026)

12 Principles

  • Be a diligent, respectful, and caring steward
  • Create a collaborative project team environment
  • Effectively engage with stakeholders
  • Focus on value
  • Recognize, evaluate, and respond to system interactions
  • Demonstrate leadership behaviors
  • Tailor based on context
  • Build quality into processes and deliverables
  • Navigate complexity
  • Optimize risk responses
  • Embrace adaptability and resiliency
  • Enable change to achieve the envisioned future state

8 Performance Domains

  • Stakeholders
  • Team
  • Development Approach and Life Cycle
  • Planning
  • Project Work
  • Delivery
  • Measurement
  • Uncertainty

Tailoring & Models

  • Tailoring guidance for predictive, agile, hybrid lifecycles
  • Models (situational leadership, communication, motivation)
  • Methods & artifacts (e.g., backlog, burndown, Gantt, retrospectives)

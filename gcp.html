GCP

Google Cloud Platform – AI-first, secure, scalable cloud infrastructure & services

Overview

Google Cloud Platform (GCP) is Google's enterprise cloud offering, known for its AI/ML leadership (Gemini models, TPUs), data & analytics strength (BigQuery, Dataflow, Looker), serverless/container-native services (Cloud Run, Cloud Functions), and security-by-design (BeyondCorp, Confidential Computing). In March 2026, GCP continues to gain share in AI workloads, hybrid/multi-cloud scenarios, and sustainability-focused enterprises. It provides IaaS, PaaS, SaaS, and managed services across compute, storage, databases, networking, security, DevOps, and AI, with tight integration between Google Workspace, Looker, and Vertex AI. GCP emphasizes open-source compatibility (Kubernetes/GKE, Istio, Knative), global network performance (premium tier), and carbon-free energy matching.

Key Value: GCP delivers unmatched AI/ML performance (TPUs, Gemini), cost-effective analytics (BigQuery), secure-by-design architecture, and sustainability leadership — enabling organizations to accelerate innovation, reduce operational complexity, and meet ESG goals in a multi-cloud world.

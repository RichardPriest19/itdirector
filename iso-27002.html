ISO/IEC 27002

ISO/IEC 27002 – international code of practice for information security controls (2022 edition)

Overview

ISO/IEC 27002:2022 is the current international standard providing a comprehensive reference for selecting, implementing, and managing information security controls. It supports ISO/IEC 27001 by offering detailed guidance on the 93 controls listed in Annex A of ISO 27001:2022. In March 2026, ISO/IEC 27002:2022 is the definitive global best-practice reference for information security controls, organized into four themes: Organizational (37 controls), People (8 controls), Physical (14 controls), and Technological (34 controls). It covers everything from policies and awareness to cryptography, secure development, threat intelligence, and cloud security — with updated focus on emerging risks (e.g., AI/ML, supply chain, zero trust). It is widely used for control selection, gap analysis, compliance mapping, and maturity improvement across industries and regulatory frameworks (GDPR, NIS2, DORA, HIPAA, CCPA, PCI-DSS, SOC 2).

Key Value: ISO/IEC 27002 provides clear, internationally recognized guidance for implementing effective, proportionate security controls — helping organizations reduce risk, achieve compliance, build resilience, and demonstrate due diligence to regulators, auditors, customers, and partners.

Core Themes & Control Categories (2022 Edition – 2026)

Organizational Controls (37)

  • Information security policies & roles
  • Supplier relationships & cloud services
  • Threat intelligence & incident management planning
  • Business continuity & compliance

People Controls (8)

  • Screening, terms & conditions of employment
  • Information security awareness & training
  • Disciplinary process & remote working

Physical Controls (14)

  • Physical & environmental security
  • Clear desk & clear screen policy
  • Access control to facilities & equipment

Technological Controls (34)

  • Authentication, access rights & privileged access
  • Cryptography, secure development & threat modeling
  • Logging, monitoring, malware protection & configuration management

Best Practices for ISO/IEC 27002 Adoption (2026)

Control Selection

  • Use risk assessment to select applicable controls
  • Document rationale in Statement of Applicability
  • Prioritize high-impact controls (e.g., access, cryptography)

Implementation & Evidence

  • Implement controls proportionately to risk
  • Maintain evidence (policies, logs, training records)
  • Use automation for monitoring & compliance checks

Continuous Improvement

  • Conduct regular control reviews & audits
  • Integrate with incident & change processes
  • Update for emerging threats (AI, supply chain, zero trust)

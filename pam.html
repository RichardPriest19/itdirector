PAM

Privileged Access Management – secure, control, and monitor privileged accounts and credentials

Overview

Privileged Access Management (PAM) is a critical cybersecurity discipline focused on controlling, monitoring, and securing accounts with elevated access rights, including administrative, root, and service accounts. PAM solutions prevent misuse of privileged credentials, reduce attack surfaces, and support regulatory compliance. As of 2026, PAM is central to **identity and access management (IAM), zero-trust security, and cybersecurity resilience** for enterprises and cloud environments.

Key Value: PAM minimises security risks associated with privileged accounts by enforcing least privilege, credential rotation, session monitoring, and detailed auditing — helping organisations protect critical assets, meet compliance standards, and reduce insider and external threats.

Core Capabilities (2026)

Access Control & Least Privilege

  • Enforce least privilege across users, admins, and service accounts
  • Granular access policies based on roles, context, and risk
  • Time-bound and just-in-time access for critical systems

Credential Management

  • Secure storage and automated rotation of passwords, keys, and certificates
  • Integration with IAM, SSO, and cloud identity providers
  • Elimination of hard-coded credentials in scripts and applications

Session Monitoring & Recording

  • Real-time monitoring of privileged sessions
  • Audit trails, keystroke logging, and session recording for compliance
  • Alerts for suspicious or high-risk activity

Analytics & Risk Management

  • Behavioural analytics for anomaly detection
  • Risk scoring and policy enforcement for privileged users
  • Reporting and dashboards for compliance audits and governance

Key Trends & Best Practices in 2026

Zero Trust & PAM Integration

  • Privileged access as a key component of zero-trust architectures
  • Conditional and risk-based access enforcement
  • Integration with endpoint detection and response (EDR) and SIEM

Cloud & Hybrid Environments

  • PAM for cloud accounts, DevOps pipelines, and SaaS admin roles
  • Secure access for remote and distributed workforce
  • Unified management across on-prem, cloud, and hybrid platforms

Automation & Privilege Lifecycle

  • Automated provisioning, de-provisioning, and credential rotation
  • Policy-driven enforcement for temporary and emergency access
  • Integration with ITSM, identity governance, and workflow tools

Best Practices for PAM Deployment (2026)

Assessment & Planning

  • Identify all privileged accounts, service accounts, and admin roles
  • Classify accounts by criticality and risk
  • Develop a phased PAM implementation strategy

Implementation & Policy Enforcement

  • Deploy secure vaults for credential storage and rotation
  • Enforce least privilege, just-in-time access, and multi-factor authentication
  • Monitor and record privileged sessions in real time

Monitoring & Continuous Improvement

  • Regularly review access policies and account activity
  • Audit and report on compliance, anomalies, and potential risks
  • Integrate PAM analytics into overall cybersecurity monitoring

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Ensure PAM solutions are integrated with IAM, zero-trust, and security monitoring
  • Oversee implementation of least privilege and credential management
  • Monitor privileged access sessions and respond to anomalies
  • Support regulatory compliance, audit, and reporting requirements
  • Coordinate with security, operations, and cloud teams for unified governance
  • Continuously assess and optimise PAM policies and technologies

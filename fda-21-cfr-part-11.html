FDA 21 CFR Part 11

Electronic Records; Electronic Signatures – FDA regulation for trustworthy electronic records & signatures

Overview

FDA 21 CFR Part 11 (often shortened to "Part 11") is the U.S. Food and Drug Administration regulation that defines the criteria under which electronic records and electronic signatures are considered trustworthy, reliable, and equivalent to paper records and handwritten signatures. In March 2026, Part 11 remains the foundational rule for all FDA-regulated electronic systems in pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, medical devices, food, dietary supplements, tobacco, and related industries. Enforcement continues to focus on **data integrity** (ALCOA+ principles), **audit trails**, **validation of computerized systems** (shift from CSV to CSA – Computer Software Assurance), **cloud/SaaS validation**, **AI/ML in regulated processes**, **remote/hybrid inspections**, **supply-chain data integrity**, and **harmonization** with EU Annex 11 and PIC/S guidance. Part 11 violations remain among the most frequently cited in FDA 483 observations, warning letters, and consent decrees. IT, quality, validation, and compliance teams are responsible for ensuring systems meet Part 11 requirements.

Key Value: Part 11 ensures electronic records and signatures are trustworthy and equivalent to paper-based processes — enabling safe digital transformation, reducing paper-based workflows, supporting audit readiness, protecting patient safety & product quality, and — when properly implemented — providing defensible evidence during FDA inspections and supporting innovation in cloud, AI, and automated systems.

Core Requirements (2026)

Electronic Records Controls (§11.10)

  • Validation of systems to ensure accuracy & reliability
  • Secure, computer-generated, time-stamped audit trails
  • Operational system checks & authority checks

Electronic Signatures (§11.50, §11.70, §11.100–§11.300)

  • Unique user IDs & passwords or biometrics
  • Linkage of signature to record
  • Non-repudiation & continuous session controls

Controls for Open & Closed Systems

  • Closed systems – internal controls sufficient
  • Open systems – additional encryption & document encoding
  • Document retention & retrieval

CSA & Risk-Based Validation

  • Shift from traditional CSV to risk-based CSA
  • Critical thinking & testing focused on patient safety
  • Cloud/SaaS & AI/ML system assurance

Key Trends & Enforcement Focus in 2026

Data Integrity & Audit Trails

  • ALCOA+ enforcement (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate + Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available)
  • Audit trail deficiencies as top 483 citation
  • Cloud audit trail validation

Cloud, SaaS & AI/ML

  • CSA guidance for cloud/SaaS GxP systems
  • AI/ML model validation & explainability
  • Data residency & international transfer controls

Inspection & Enforcement

  • Remote & hybrid inspections continuing
  • Warning letters & consent decrees for data integrity
  • Supply-chain & third-party vendor focus

Best Practices for Part 11 Compliance (2026)

Validation & CSA

  • Adopt risk-based CSA approach (FDA 2022 guidance)
  • Focus testing on critical requirements & patient safety
  • Validate cloud/SaaS & AI/ML systems

Data Integrity & Audit Trails

  • Implement ALCOA+ compliant audit trails
  • Enforce user authentication & access controls
  • Conduct periodic data integrity assessments

Inspection Readiness

  • Maintain inspection readiness & mock audits
  • Document validation, change control & deviations
  • Train staff on Part 11 & data integrity

IT Director / Quality / Validation Responsibilities

  • Champion Part 11 & data integrity as patient safety foundation
  • Approve CSA/validation strategy & risk assessments
  • Ensure audit trails, electronic signatures & access controls
  • Oversee cloud/SaaS/AI/ML GxP system assurance
  • Monitor inspection readiness & regulatory trends
  • Drive integration with QMS, ERP & supply-chain systems
  • Align Part 11 with GxP, ESG & digital transformation goals

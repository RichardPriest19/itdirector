MITRE ATT&CK

MITRE ATT&CK – globally recognized knowledge base of adversary tactics, techniques & procedures

Overview

MITRE ATT&CK (Adversarial Tactics, Techniques, and Common Knowledge) is the open-source, globally adopted framework that catalogs real-world cyber adversary behaviors across the attack lifecycle — from initial access to impact. In March 2026, **ATT&CK v16** is the current version, with expanded matrices covering **Enterprise**, **Mobile**, **ICS/OT**, **Cloud**, **PRE-ATT&CK**, and new sub-techniques for **AI/ML-enabled attacks**, **ransomware evolution**, **living-off-the-land binaries (LOLBins)**, **supply-chain compromise**, **post-exploitation persistence**, and **cloud-native behaviors** (AWS, Azure, GCP). It is the de facto standard for **threat-informed defense**, **detection engineering**, **threat hunting**, **red/blue/purple teaming**, **SOC maturity**, **incident response playbooks**, **cyber insurance underwriting**, **regulatory mapping** (DORA, NIS2, SEC 4-hour disclosure, CMMC 2.0), and **AI-driven TTP correlation**. ATT&CK is integrated into nearly every major security platform (Splunk, Elastic, Microsoft Sentinel, CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Cortex XDR, IBM QRadar, Google Chronicle, Rapid7 InsightIDR) and used by MITRE Engenuity for **ATT&CK Evaluations** and **ATT&CK Sightings**.

Key Value: MITRE ATT&CK provides a common language, structured knowledge base, and adversary-informed lens for cybersecurity — enabling threat-informed detection, prioritized defenses, effective threat hunting, red team emulation, incident response playbooks, regulatory compliance mapping, cyber insurance justification, and — with AI integration — automated TTP correlation, gap analysis, and proactive defense against known and emerging adversary behaviors.

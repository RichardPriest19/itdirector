IaaS

Infrastructure as a Service – on-demand virtualized compute, storage & networking in the cloud

Overview

Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) provides on-demand access to virtualized computing resources — servers, storage, networking, load balancers, firewalls — over the internet, with the provider managing the underlying physical infrastructure. In March 2026, IaaS remains the foundational cloud layer for enterprises, supporting **hybrid/multi-cloud strategies**, **AI/ML acceleration** (GPU/TPU clusters, high-memory VMs), **confidential computing** (encrypted-in-use VMs), **sustainability initiatives** (carbon-aware scheduling, renewable energy matching), **FinOps optimization** (spot/preemptible instances, commitment discounts), **zero-trust infrastructure**, and **edge IaaS** for low-latency workloads. Major providers include **AWS EC2**, **Azure Virtual Machines**, **Google Compute Engine**, **Oracle Cloud Infrastructure**, **IBM Cloud**, **Alibaba Cloud ECS**, and specialized players (CoreWeave, Lambda Labs for AI compute).

Key Value: IaaS delivers flexible, scalable, pay-as-you-go infrastructure with global reach, high availability, and rapid provisioning — enabling organizations to shift CapEx to OpEx, accelerate innovation (especially AI/ML), support hybrid/multi-cloud resilience, and achieve cost & sustainability goals without owning data centers.

Core Components & Services (2026)

Compute

  • Virtual machines (VMs) – general-purpose, GPU/TPU, high-memory, Arm-based
  • Spot/preemptible instances & commitment discounts
  • Confidential VMs & secure enclaves

Storage

  • Block (SSD/HDD), object, file, archival storage
  • Snapshot & backup services
  • AI-optimized storage for ML training datasets

Networking

  • Virtual networks, subnets, load balancers, firewalls
  • Global VPC peering & direct connect
  • Edge locations & CDN integration

Security & Governance

  • Identity & access (IAM roles, workload identity)
  • Encryption at rest/in transit/in use
  • Compliance certifications (FedRAMP, ISO 27001, SOC 2)

Best Practices for IaaS Adoption (2026)

Architecture & Security

  • Implement zero-trust from day one (workload identity, encryption)
  • Use confidential computing for sensitive workloads
  • Design for multi-cloud & hybrid resilience

Cost & Governance

  • Adopt FinOps practices & anomaly detection
  • Use spot/preemptible instances for non-critical workloads
  • Implement tagging & policy enforcement

AI & Edge Strategy

  • Leverage GPU/TPU clusters for AI/ML
  • Plan edge IaaS for low-latency use cases
  • Monitor carbon footprint & sustainability metrics

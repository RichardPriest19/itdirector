EnCE

EnCase Certified Examiner – OpenText certification validating expert proficiency in EnCase forensic software

Overview

The EnCase Certified Examiner (EnCE) is awarded by OpenText (formerly Guidance Software) and certifies proficiency in using the EnCase forensic platform for digital investigations. EnCE validates both legal knowledge of computer forensics and technical mastery of EnCase tools for evidence acquisition, analysis, and reporting — and is widely recognised in law enforcement, legal, and corporate investigation communities.

Key Value: EnCE is the gold standard certification for EnCase platform users — recognised by law enforcement agencies, courts, and corporate investigation teams worldwide as evidence of investigative competency and tool mastery in one of the most widely deployed forensic platforms globally.

Core Competency Areas

Digital Forensics Fundamentals

  • Apply forensic sound acquisition principles — write blocking, hashing, and chain of custody
  • Understand file system structures — NTFS, FAT, exFAT, ext4, and HFS+
  • Apply legal standards for digital evidence admissibility in civil and criminal proceedings
  • Understand data recovery concepts — deleted files, unallocated space, and file carving

EnCase Platform Proficiency

  • Configure and operate EnCase Forensic for evidence acquisition and analysis
  • Create, verify, and manage evidence files in EnCase format (E01, Ex01)
  • Build and execute EnScript programmes for automated investigation workflows
  • Use EnCase Portable, EnCase eDiscovery, and EnCase Endpoint Investigator

Evidence Analysis & Investigation

  • Search evidence files using keyword, hash, and signature-based methods
  • Analyse Registry, event logs, browser history, email, and user activity artefacts
  • Perform timeline analysis and reconstruct user and system activity sequences
  • Generate case reports and export findings in court-admissible formats

Legal & Compliance Framework

  • Apply applicable legal frameworks for computer forensics investigations in civil, criminal, and employment contexts
  • Understand eDiscovery requirements and electronically stored information (ESI) standards
  • Ensure investigations comply with privacy laws and organisational policies

Exam Details

  • Phase 1: Written examination — 180 multiple-choice questions covering forensic principles and EnCase knowledge
  • Phase 2: Practical examination — candidates investigate a provided evidence file and submit findings
  • Both phases must be passed to achieve EnCE certification
  • Prerequisite: Submission of an application demonstrating relevant professional experience
  • Renewal: Continuing education and periodic revalidation required by OpenText

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Deploy EnCE-certified examiners in digital forensics, e-discovery, and incident investigation functions
  • Maintain EnCase software licences as part of the organisation’s forensic and legal response capability
  • Ensure EnCE-qualified staff lead digital investigations to maintain evidence admissibility
  • Coordinate EnCE examiners with legal counsel for e-discovery requests, litigation support, and regulatory enquiries

TORIT

Target Operating Model for IT – blueprint for future-state IT organization, processes, technology, and service delivery

Overview

TORIT (Target Operating Model for IT) defines how the IT function will operate in the future — including structure, capabilities, processes, governance, and technology enablement.

Key Elements

  • IT strategy alignment with business objectives
  • Organization design and team topology
  • Service catalog, operating processes, and SLAs
  • Technology roadmap and platform strategy
  • Governance, risk, and performance measurement

Best Practices 2026

  • Develop TORIT as part of digital transformation initiatives
  • Use iterative design with strong stakeholder engagement
  • Align with frameworks such as ITIL 4, COBIT, and SIAM
  • Include metrics for success (DORA, KPIs, OKRs)
  • Plan for continuous evolution rather than one-time change

ISO/IEC 27018

ISO/IEC 27018 – code of practice for protection of personally identifiable information (PII) in public cloud environments

Overview

ISO/IEC 27018:2019 is the international code of practice specifically addressing the protection of personally identifiable information (PII) processed by public cloud service providers. It extends ISO/IEC 27002 controls with cloud-specific PII safeguards, focusing on transparency, accountability, data minimization, and customer control. In March 2026, ISO/IEC 27018 remains the global benchmark for PII protection in public clouds, frequently required in vendor contracts, data processing agreements (DPAs), and privacy impact assessments (PIAs/DPIAs). It aligns closely with ISO/IEC 27701 (privacy information management), GDPR Article 28 requirements, and emerging regulations (e.g., EU Cloud Code of Conduct, APPI in Japan, LGPD in Brazil). Major cloud providers (AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle, Alibaba) publish ISO 27018 compliance attestations and mappings to demonstrate adherence.

Key Value: ISO/IEC 27018 provides a clear, auditable framework for protecting PII in public clouds, builds customer trust, simplifies vendor risk assessments, supports global compliance (GDPR, CCPA, etc.), and reduces legal exposure when using shared cloud infrastructure.

Core Principles & Controls (2026)

Key Principles

  • Transparency & customer control over PII processing
  • Data minimization & purpose limitation in cloud
  • Sub-processor transparency & consent
  • Incident notification & breach response obligations

Cloud-Specific Controls (Annex A extensions)

  • A.5.1.1 – PII controller informs provider of legal obligations
  • A.6.1.1 – Sub-processor disclosure & approval
  • A.8.2.1 – PII data segregation & isolation
  • A.10.1.1 – Customer audit rights & provider cooperation
  • A.12.3.1 – Data portability & return/destruction at contract end

Integration with ISO 27001/27701

  • Extends ISO 27001 Annex A with PII-specific controls
  • Harmonized with ISO/IEC 27701 privacy management system
  • Supports joint certification (27001 + 27018 + 27701)

Key Adoption & Trends in 2026

Cloud Provider Compliance

  • AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle publish ISO 27018 SoC reports
  • Increased contractual commitments in DPAs & SLAs
  • Customer demand for 27018 attestation in RFPs

Regulatory Alignment

  • Strong mapping to GDPR Art. 28 & Schrems II requirements
  • Referenced in CCPA/CPRA, LGPD, PDPA, APPI compliance
  • Supports cross-border data transfer assessments

Emerging Requirements

  • Integration with Zero Trust & SSPM for cloud PII
  • Focus on AI/ML workloads processing PII (e.g., training data)
  • Third-party risk management & continuous monitoring

Best Practices for ISO/IEC 27018 Adoption (2026)

Cloud Vendor Selection

  • Require ISO 27018 certification or equivalent mapping
  • Include 27018 controls in DPAs & audit rights
  • Verify sub-processor transparency & consent mechanisms

Internal Governance

  • Conduct PII-specific risk assessments for cloud workloads
  • Implement data classification & tagging for PII
  • Enable customer-controlled encryption & key management

Monitoring & Assurance

  • Regularly review provider compliance reports & attestations
  • Perform joint audits or third-party assessments
  • Monitor for unauthorized PII processing & incident notifications

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion ISO/IEC 27018 as core cloud PII protection standard
  • Approve vendor selection criteria & contractual controls
  • Ensure PII risk assessments & data protection impact assessments
  • Oversee compliance mapping (GDPR, CCPA, LGPD, etc.)
  • Monitor cloud provider attestations & incident notifications
  • Drive Zero Trust & encryption adoption for cloud PII
  • Plan for AI/ML workloads & emerging cloud privacy rules

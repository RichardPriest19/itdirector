CRISC

Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control – ISACA certification for IT risk identification, assessment, and management

Overview

The Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control (CRISC) is awarded by ISACA and is designed for IT and business professionals who identify and manage risks through the development, implementation, and maintenance of information systems controls. CRISC is particularly valued in GRC, internal audit, and enterprise risk management functions where IT risk must be quantified, communicated, and mitigated at an organisational level.

Key Value: CRISC is the most recognised certification specifically focused on IT risk management and control — bridging the gap between IT, business risk, and governance functions and demonstrating expertise in translating IT risk into business impact language.

Core Exam Domains

Governance

  • Align IT risk management with enterprise risk management (ERM) frameworks and organisational strategy
  • Establish IT risk governance structures, policies, and accountability across the organisation
  • Apply COBIT, ISO 31000, and NIST RMF as frameworks for IT risk governance
  • Communicate risk posture and risk appetite to executives, boards, and business stakeholders

IT Risk Assessment

  • Identify threats, vulnerabilities, and risk scenarios across the IT environment
  • Conduct qualitative and quantitative risk assessments — likelihood, impact, and inherent risk
  • Maintain a current and comprehensive IT risk register
  • Analyse risk in the context of business processes, data assets, and critical systems

Risk Response & Reporting

  • Develop and implement risk treatment plans — mitigation, transfer, acceptance, and avoidance
  • Monitor key risk indicators (KRIs) to detect emerging risks and control degradation
  • Report risk status to governance bodies using dashboards, heat maps, and executive summaries
  • Evaluate the residual risk after controls are applied and adjust treatment as needed

Information Technology & Security

  • Assess IT controls for effectiveness — preventive, detective, and corrective
  • Evaluate security architecture, access controls, and data protection measures as risk controls
  • Integrate risk management into IT project delivery, change management, and procurement

Exam Details

  • 150 multiple-choice questions over 4 hours
  • Passing score: 450 out of 800 (scaled)
  • Prerequisite: 3 years of IT risk management and IS control experience across CRISC domains
  • Renewal: 120 CPE credits over 3 years with a minimum of 20 CPE annually

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Require CRISC for IT risk managers, GRC leads, and compliance programme owners
  • Use CRISC-qualified staff to lead IT risk assessments, control evaluations, and risk reporting
  • Integrate CRISC-governed risk management into IT strategy, programme delivery, and vendor management
  • Ensure IT risk reporting to the board is structured, evidenced, and aligned with CRISC standards

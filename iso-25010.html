ISO/IEC 25010 series

ISO/IEC 25010 – international standards for system & software quality models & evaluation (SQuaRE)

Overview

The ISO/IEC 25010 series (part of the SQuaRE framework) defines comprehensive quality models for systems and software products. The core standards are **ISO/IEC 25010:2011** (original) and **ISO/IEC 25010:2023** (current edition), which provide two main models: - **Product quality model** — eight characteristics (functional suitability, performance efficiency, compatibility, usability, reliability, security, maintainability, portability) and associated sub-characteristics - **Quality-in-use model** — five characteristics (effectiveness, efficiency, satisfaction, freedom from risk, context coverage) In March 2026, ISO/IEC 25010:2023 is the globally accepted reference for defining, measuring, and evaluating software/system quality. It is widely used in software development contracts, quality assurance, testing strategies, DevOps maturity models, regulatory compliance (ISO 13485 medical devices, automotive SPICE, aerospace DO-178C), public procurement, and benchmarking. It supports agile, DevOps, and AI/ML software quality evaluation and is frequently referenced alongside ISO/IEC 12207 (software lifecycle) and ISO/IEC 25023 (measurement).

Key Value: ISO/IEC 25010 provides a clear, internationally agreed quality model that enables objective specification, measurement, and evaluation of software/system quality — improving stakeholder alignment, reducing rework, supporting regulatory compliance, and driving higher-quality outcomes across the entire software lifecycle.

Core Quality Models (2023 Edition – 2026)

Product Quality Model – 8 Characteristics

  • Functional suitability (completeness, correctness, appropriateness)
  • Performance efficiency (time behaviour, resource utilization, capacity)
  • Compatibility (co-existence, interoperability)
  • Usability (appropriateness recognizability, learnability, operability, user error protection, user interface aesthetics, accessibility)
  • Reliability (maturity, availability, fault tolerance, recoverability)
  • Security (confidentiality, integrity, non-repudiation, accountability, authenticity)
  • Maintainability (modularity, reusability, analysability, modifiability, testability)
  • Portability (adaptability, installability, replaceability)

Quality-in-Use Model – 5 Characteristics

  • Effectiveness
  • Efficiency
  • Satisfaction (usefulness, trust, pleasure, comfort)
  • Freedom from risk (economic risk mitigation, health & safety risk mitigation, environmental risk mitigation)
  • Context coverage (context completeness, flexibility)

Measurement & Evaluation

  • Aligned with ISO/IEC 25023 (measurement of quality)
  • Supports internal & external quality evaluation
  • Used in acceptance criteria, testing, benchmarking

Key Adoption & Trends in 2026

Regulatory & Compliance Use

  • Referenced in ISO 13485 (medical devices), automotive SPICE, aerospace DO-178C
  • Used in public procurement quality requirements
  • Supports GDPR, CCPA, EU AI Act non-functional quality obligations

AI/ML & Modern Software

  • Quality models applied to AI systems (robustness, fairness, explainability)
  • Support for DevSecOps & secure-by-design quality
  • Integration with ISO/IEC 42001 (AI management system)

Measurement & Agility

  • Quality metrics in CI/CD pipelines & DevOps
  • Tailoring for agile & hybrid lifecycles
  • Focus on user experience & value-in-use

Best Practices for ISO/IEC 25010 Adoption (2026)

Quality Specification

  • Define quality requirements using 25010 characteristics
  • Include in contracts, acceptance criteria & test plans
  • Prioritize security, usability, performance for modern systems

Measurement & Evaluation

  • Use ISO/IEC 25023 for quantitative measures
  • Conduct internal & external quality evaluations
  • Integrate quality gates in DevOps pipelines

Continuous Improvement

  • Track quality metrics over time & across releases
  • Align with ISO/IEC 12207 lifecycle processes
  • Update quality models for AI & emerging technologies

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion ISO/IEC 25010 as core software quality reference
  • Approve quality requirements & acceptance criteria
  • Ensure quality-in-use & security focus in projects
  • Oversee quality measurement, testing & compliance
  • Monitor software quality KPIs & maturity
  • Drive integration with DevOps, AI & regulatory needs
  • Align with ISO 27001, ITIL 4 & lifecycle standards

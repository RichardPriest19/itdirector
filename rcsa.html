RCSA (Risk and Control Self-Assessment)

Structured process where business units and IT teams identify, assess, and manage risks along with the effectiveness of their own internal controls

Overview

RCSA is a key component of enterprise risk management (ERM) frameworks. It empowers first-line teams (business and IT owners) to self-assess risks and controls rather than relying solely on second or third lines of defense. It is widely used in banking, financial services, and regulated industries.

Core Objectives

  • Identify and document key risks within processes or systems
  • Evaluate the design and operating effectiveness of existing controls
  • Determine residual risk levels after applying controls
  • Identify control gaps and assign remediation actions with owners and deadlines
  • Provide evidence for regulatory compliance and internal audit

Typical RCSA Process

  1. Define scope (processes, systems, or departments)
  2. Identify inherent risks
  3. Map and assess controls (preventive, detective, corrective)
  4. Rate control effectiveness (using a scoring matrix)
  5. Calculate residual risk
  6. Document action plans for gaps
  7. Review and sign-off by process owners

IT Director / CISO Role in RCSA

IT teams are usually responsible for RCSA coverage of key domains such as:

  • IT General Controls (ITGC)
  • Cybersecurity and information security controls
  • Change management and access management
  • Disaster recovery and business continuity
  • Third-party / vendor risk management

Relation to Other Frameworks

RCSA is a core element of the **Three Lines of Defense** model. It supports SOX compliance, SOC 2 reporting, ISO 27001, and regulatory requirements such as DORA, Basel III, and operational resilience mandates.

CEH

Certified Ethical Hacker – EC-Council certification for offensive security, penetration testing, and ethical hacking techniques

Overview

The Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH) is a globally recognised certification awarded by EC-Council. It validates a professional's ability to think and act like a malicious hacker — legally and ethically — in order to assess and strengthen an organisation's security posture. CEH covers hacking methodologies, attack techniques, and the tools used by threat actors, equipping defenders with offensive knowledge to identify and remediate vulnerabilities before they are exploited.

Key Value: CEH is one of the most widely recognised offensive security certifications globally, accepted by employers across government, defence, finance, and technology sectors as evidence of hands-on ethical hacking competency.

Core Exam Domains (CEH v13 / 2026)

Reconnaissance & Footprinting

  • Perform passive and active reconnaissance using OSINT, DNS enumeration, and network scanning
  • Use tools including Maltego, Shodan, Recon-ng, and Nmap for target profiling
  • Map network topology, identify open ports, and fingerprint operating systems and services

Vulnerability Assessment & Exploitation

  • Conduct system, web application, and network vulnerability scanning
  • Exploit known CVEs, misconfigurations, and weak credentials using Metasploit and manual techniques
  • Perform SQL injection, XSS, CSRF, and other OWASP Top 10 web application attacks
  • Apply privilege escalation techniques on Windows and Linux systems

Social Engineering & Phishing

  • Design and execute phishing campaigns, pretexting, and vishing simulations
  • Use SET (Social Engineering Toolkit) for targeted attack simulations
  • Assess human-layer vulnerabilities and measure security awareness effectiveness

Post-Exploitation & Evasion

  • Maintain access through persistence mechanisms, backdoors, and Trojans
  • Cover tracks by clearing logs, manipulating timestamps, and evading endpoint detection
  • Exfiltrate data using covert channels and encrypted communications

Exam Details

  • Exam code: 312-50 — 125 multiple-choice questions over 4 hours
  • Passing score: 70% (varies by version and question bank)
  • CEH Practical: separate 6-hour hands-on lab exam available for full practitioner designation
  • Prerequisite: 2 years of information security experience or completion of official EC-Council training
  • Renewal: 120 EC-Council Continuing Education (ECE) credits every 3 years

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Deploy CEH-certified professionals in red team, penetration testing, and vulnerability management roles
  • Commission regular ethical hacking engagements to validate security controls and identify gaps
  • Use CEH findings to prioritise remediation and inform risk management decisions
  • Ensure rules of engagement, scoping, and legal authorisation are documented for all ethical hacking activities

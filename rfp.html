RFP

Request for Proposal – formal procurement document to solicit vendor bids for complex IT projects & services

Overview

A Request for Proposal (RFP) is a formal document issued by an organization to solicit detailed proposals from vendors/suppliers for complex products, services, or solutions — typically involving significant cost, customization, or strategic impact. In March 2026, RFPs remain a core procurement mechanism for enterprise software, cloud platforms, cybersecurity services, consulting, outsourcing, and digital transformation initiatives. Modern RFPs emphasize **digital/eRFP platforms** (Ariba, Coupa, Jaggaer, Ivalua), **AI-assisted RFP creation & response scoring**, **cybersecurity & privacy questionnaires** (NIS2/DORA, ISO 27001/27701, SOC 2), **ESG & sustainability criteria**, **value-based evaluation** (TCO, ROI, outcomes), and **vendor risk assessment** (supply-chain, data residency, exit strategy). They are increasingly outcome-focused rather than feature checklists.

Key Value: A well-structured RFP ensures competitive, transparent procurement, aligns vendor solutions to strategic needs, mitigates risk through due diligence, drives best value (price + quality + outcomes), and provides defensible selection decisions for auditors, regulators, and stakeholders.

Core RFP Phases & Components (2026)

Preparation & Drafting

  • Define business requirements, scope, success criteria & TCO model
  • Include cybersecurity/privacy (NIS2/DORA, ISO 27017/27701), ESG, exit clauses
  • AI-assisted RFP drafting & question generation

Issuance & Response

  • Use eRFP platforms for distribution & collaboration
  • Require detailed responses (technical, commercial, risk, references)
  • Conduct Q&A sessions & clarifications

Evaluation & Selection

  • Weighted scoring (value, price, risk, references, innovation)
  • AI-assisted proposal scoring & red-flag detection
  • Proof-of-concept/pilot & reference checks

Negotiation & Award

  • Final negotiation (pricing, SLAs, exit terms)
  • Contract award & transition planning
  • Post-award governance & vendor performance tracking

Best Practices for RFP Management (2026)

RFP Creation

  • Be outcome-focused, not feature checklist
  • Include clear evaluation criteria & weighting
  • Require detailed risk, compliance & exit plans

Evaluation & Scoring

  • Use cross-functional evaluation team & consistent scoring
  • Leverage AI for initial screening & red-flag detection
  • Conduct demos/PoCs for shortlisted vendors

Post-Award

  • Establish governance & performance tracking
  • Monitor SLA, value realization & risk
  • Conduct lessons learned & vendor scorecard

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion RFP as strategic procurement tool
  • Approve RFP scope, requirements & evaluation criteria
  • Ensure cybersecurity/privacy/ESG clauses & vendor risk assessment
  • Oversee due diligence, shortlisting & negotiation
  • Monitor contract award, transition & value capture
  • Drive AI-assisted RFP & evaluation processes
  • Align RFP outcomes with IT strategy & governance

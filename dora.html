DORA

Digital Operational Resilience Act – EU regulation for ICT risk management in financial sector

Overview

The Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA – Regulation (EU) 2022/2554) is the European Union's harmonized framework for strengthening the digital operational resilience of financial entities (banks, insurers, investment firms, crypto-asset service providers, central counterparties, etc.) and their critical ICT third-party providers. Fully enforceable since January 17, 2025, in March 2026 DORA is one of the most impactful pieces of financial-sector cybersecurity regulation globally — requiring **ICT risk management frameworks**, **incident reporting** (major incidents within 4 hours initial notification), **resilience testing** (including threat-led penetration testing – TLPT), **third-party risk management** (especially for critical cloud & ICT providers), **information sharing**, and **oversight** of CTPPs by the European Supervisory Authorities (EBA, EIOPA, ESMA). Non-compliance carries severe fines (up to 2% of global turnover or €10 million). IT, cybersecurity, compliance, and third-party risk teams are heavily involved in implementation, annual reviews, and regulatory reporting.

Key Value: DORA forces financial entities to build genuine ICT resilience — mandating proactive risk management, rapid incident reporting, rigorous testing, strict third-party oversight, and information sharing — ultimately reducing systemic cyber risk, improving operational continuity, aligning cybersecurity with business resilience, and providing a harmonized, high-standard framework across the EU financial sector.

Core Pillars & Requirements (2026)

ICT Risk Management

  • Comprehensive ICT risk framework integrated into enterprise risk
  • Annual reviews, policies, procedures & controls
  • Business continuity & disaster recovery planning

Incident Reporting

  • Major ICT-related incidents: initial notification ≤ 4 hours
  • Intermediate report ≤ 72 hours, final report ≤ 1 month
  • Voluntary notification of significant cyber threats

Resilience Testing

  • Annual digital operational resilience testing
  • Threat-led penetration testing (TLPT) every 3 years for critical entities
  • Testing of third-party providers

Third-Party Risk & Oversight

  • Critical ICT third-party providers (CTPPs) designated by ESAs
  • Contractual requirements, exit strategies, audits
  • Direct supervisory powers over CTPPs

Key Trends & Focus Areas in 2026

Implementation & Enforcement

  • Full enforcement year – first major incident reports & TLPT cycles
  • ESA oversight of CTPPs (cloud hyperscalers, critical SaaS)
  • Harmonization with NIS2 & DORA overlap management

AI & Emerging Risks

  • AI/ML risk classification & controls
  • Generative AI usage in financial services under DORA
  • Supply-chain & cloud concentration risk focus

Cross-Border & Global Alignment

  • Alignment with UK Operational Resilience rules
  • Impact on non-EU subsidiaries & third parties
  • Global banks managing DORA + Basel + local rules

Best Practices for DORA Compliance (2026)

Risk Management & Governance

  • Integrate ICT risk into enterprise risk framework
  • Conduct annual risk assessments & gap analyses
  • Establish DORA steering committee & reporting

Incident & Testing

  • Implement 4-hour major incident notification process
  • Prepare for TLPT & advanced resilience testing
  • Automate incident classification & reporting

Third-Party & Oversight

  • Map critical ICT providers & contractual compliance
  • Conduct exit planning & concentration risk assessments
  • Monitor ESA designations & oversight actions

IT Director / CISO Responsibilities

  • Lead DORA implementation & ICT risk management framework
  • Approve incident reporting & resilience testing plans
  • Ensure third-party risk & CTPP contractual compliance
  • Oversee DORA governance, audits & regulatory reporting
  • Monitor ICT risk posture, incidents & testing outcomes
  • Drive alignment with NIS2, GDPR, AI Act & global standards
  • Integrate DORA into cyber insurance & board reporting

BCP (Business Continuity Planning)

Process of developing systems, policies, and procedures to ensure critical business functions can continue during and after a disruption

Overview

Business Continuity Planning (BCP) is a proactive, organization-wide discipline focused on identifying potential threats to operations and building resilience so that critical functions can continue (or be rapidly restored) during and after disruptive events such as cyberattacks, natural disasters, supply chain failures, or pandemics.

Core Components of a BCP

  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA) – Identifies critical processes, dependencies, and recovery time objectives (RTO/RPO)
  • Risk Assessment – Evaluates likelihood and impact of various threats
  • Recovery Strategies – Defines how critical functions will be maintained (e.g., failover, alternate sites, manual workarounds)
  • Incident Response & Crisis Management – Clear escalation paths and decision-making frameworks
  • Testing & Exercises – Tabletop, simulation, and full-scale tests to validate plans
  • Maintenance & Review – Regular updates to reflect organizational changes

Key Metrics

  • RTO (Recovery Time Objective) – Maximum acceptable downtime for a process
  • RPO (Recovery Point Objective) – Maximum acceptable data loss
  • MTPD (Maximum Tolerable Period of Disruption) – Point at which the organization suffers irreversible damage

Relation to Other Disciplines

BCP is closely linked with:

  • Disaster Recovery (DR) – Technical recovery of systems and data
  • Incident Response & Crisis Management
  • IT Service Continuity Management (part of ITIL)
  • Regulatory requirements (DORA, NIS2, FCA, etc.)

IT Director Responsibilities

As an IT leader, you are typically accountable for ensuring that:

  • Critical IT systems and data have appropriate recovery strategies
  • IT DR plans are tested regularly and aligned with business BIA
  • Cloud, hybrid, and on-premises environments meet defined RTO/RPO targets
  • BCP is integrated into change management, vendor management, and project governance

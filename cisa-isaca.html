CISA

Certified Information Systems Auditor – ISACA certification for IT audit, control, and assurance professionals

Overview

The Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA) is awarded by ISACA and is the globally recognised standard for IT audit, assurance, and control professionals. It validates expertise in auditing information systems, assessing vulnerabilities, reporting on compliance, and implementing and maintaining controls within enterprise environments. CISA is held by over 170,000 professionals worldwide and is widely required by regulators, auditors, and enterprise organisations.

Key Value: CISA is the definitive credential for IT audit and assurance professionals — demonstrating the knowledge and skills to evaluate information systems controls, assess risk, and provide independent assurance to boards, regulators, and senior management.

Core Exam Domains

Information System Auditing Process

  • Plan and execute IS audits in accordance with ISACA IS Audit Standards and risk-based methodologies
  • Evaluate audit evidence, conduct interviews, and document findings objectively
  • Report audit results and recommendations to management and governance bodies
  • Follow up on audit findings to verify remediation and control improvements

Governance & Management of IT

  • Assess IT governance frameworks including COBIT, ITIL, and ISO/IEC 38500
  • Evaluate IT strategic planning, HR management, and organisational structures
  • Review IT policies, procedures, and standards for alignment with business objectives
  • Assess IT risk management practices and organisational risk appetite

Information Systems Acquisition, Development & Implementation

  • Audit system development lifecycles, project management, and change control processes
  • Evaluate application controls, input validation, and data integrity mechanisms
  • Assess third-party development, cloud migration, and acquisition due diligence processes

Information Systems Operations & Business Resilience

  • Evaluate IT operations including incident management, problem management, and change management
  • Assess business continuity and disaster recovery plans against RTO/RPO objectives
  • Review backup and recovery procedures, and data retention practices

Protection of Information Assets

  • Evaluate logical and physical access controls, encryption, and identity management
  • Assess network security architecture, perimeter controls, and monitoring capabilities
  • Review data classification, privacy controls, and regulatory compliance posture

Exam Details

  • 150 multiple-choice questions over 4 hours
  • Passing score: 450 out of 800 (scaled)
  • Prerequisite: 5 years of professional IT audit, control, or security work experience (substitutions allowed)
  • Renewal: 120 CPE credits over 3 years, with a minimum of 20 CPE per year

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Require CISA for internal IT audit leads, GRC managers, and compliance assurance roles
  • Engage CISA-qualified professionals for third-party IT audits and regulatory examination support
  • Ensure IT audit plans are risk-based, independent, and aligned with ISACA IS Audit Standards
  • Use CISA audit findings to drive control improvements and governance maturity

CIPT

Certified Information Privacy Technologist – IAPP certification for IT professionals implementing privacy in technology and systems

Overview

The Certified Information Privacy Technologist (CIPT) is awarded by the International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP). It is specifically designed for IT, software, and data professionals who are responsible for embedding privacy into technology design, systems architecture, and product development. CIPT bridges the gap between privacy law (CIPP) and technical implementation, making it the essential credential for privacy engineers, developers, and security architects.

Key Value: CIPT is the only major professional certification that specifically addresses privacy from a technology and engineering perspective — equipping IT professionals to build privacy-respecting systems rather than simply complying with policy mandates after the fact.

Core Exam Domains

Privacy in Technology Fundamentals

  • Understand the relationship between privacy law, technical standards, and engineering practice
  • Apply core privacy concepts — data minimisation, purpose limitation, and storage limitation — at the system design level
  • Identify privacy risks in software architectures, data flows, and third-party integrations

Privacy by Design & Engineering

  • Implement privacy-by-design principles across the software development lifecycle (SDLC)
  • Apply data anonymisation, pseudonymisation, and differential privacy techniques
  • Design consent management, data subject rights workflows, and preference centres into applications
  • Conduct privacy-focused threat modelling and data flow mapping for new systems

Privacy in Emerging Technologies

  • Address privacy risks in AI/ML systems — training data minimisation, model transparency, and bias mitigation
  • Apply privacy controls for IoT, wearables, and connected device environments
  • Manage privacy challenges in cloud architectures — data residency, tenant isolation, and access control
  • Implement privacy-preserving analytics and federated learning approaches

Privacy Operations for Technologists

  • Support DPIAs from a technical perspective — identifying and documenting technical risk mitigations
  • Integrate privacy into DevSecOps pipelines and CI/CD workflows
  • Manage cookie compliance, tracking technologies, and consent frameworks in web and mobile applications

Exam Details

  • 90 multiple-choice questions over 2.5 hours
  • Designed for IT, engineering, and product development professionals — no legal background required
  • Renewal: 20 CPE credits annually with IAPP membership
  • Frequently paired with CIPP/E or CIPM for a complete privacy professional skill set

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Require CIPT for software architects, data engineers, and developers working on personal data processing systems
  • Embed CIPT competencies into SDLC processes, design review gates, and DevSecOps pipelines
  • Use CIPT-qualified staff to lead privacy engineering reviews, DPIA technical assessments, and vendor evaluations
  • Promote CIPT as the technical counterpart to the DPO’s CIPP/E legal knowledge within the privacy function

CCB

Change Control Board – governance body that reviews, approves & oversees changes to IT systems & processes

Overview

The Change Control Board (CCB), also known as the Change Advisory Board (CAB) in ITIL terminology, is a cross-functional governance body responsible for reviewing, assessing risk, approving/rejecting, prioritizing, and overseeing changes to production IT systems, applications, infrastructure, configurations, code, and operational processes. In March 2026, the CCB remains a critical control in mature IT, DevOps, platform engineering, and regulated environments — ensuring changes are deliberate, risk-managed, compliant, and aligned with business objectives. It integrates **risk assessment**, **impact analysis**, **rollback/contingency planning**, **compliance alignment** (GxP, FDA 21 CFR Part 11, DORA, NIS2, PCI-DSS, ISO 27001), **automation & policy-as-code** enforcement, **AI-assisted change risk scoring**, **zero-downtime change patterns** (blue-green, canary, feature flags), and mandatory **post-change review** (PIR). The CCB typically includes representatives from IT operations, development, security, compliance, business, and change management.

Key Value: The CCB acts as a gatekeeper and risk mitigator — preventing unauthorized or high-risk changes, ensuring compliance & auditability, minimizing production incidents & downtime, enabling safe velocity in DevOps environments, providing clear accountability, and turning change into a controlled, predictable, and auditable process.

Core CCB Responsibilities & Process (2026)

Change Review & Approval

  • Assess change requests (standard, normal, emergency)
  • Evaluate risk, impact, business justification & rollback plan
  • Approve, reject, defer, or request rework

Risk & Impact Assessment

  • Risk scoring (likelihood × impact)
  • Dependency mapping & blast radius analysis
  • AI-assisted risk prediction & anomaly detection

Compliance & Auditability

  • Ensure alignment with regulatory & policy requirements
  • Maintain change audit trail & documentation
  • Enforce segregation of duties & approval thresholds

Post-Change Oversight

  • Post-implementation review (PIR) for all significant changes
  • Track incidents caused by changes
  • Lessons learned & process improvement

Key Trends & Best Practices in 2026

Automation & Policy-as-Code

  • Policy-as-code enforcement in CI/CD pipelines
  • Automated change risk scoring & approval gates
  • Shift-left change governance in DevOps

Zero-Downtime & Safe Velocity

  • Blue-green, canary, feature-flag deployments
  • Progressive delivery & automated rollback
  • AI-driven change impact prediction

Regulatory & Compliance Pressure

  • DORA, NIS2, FDA 21 CFR Part 11, GxP change control
  • Cyber insurance requirements for change governance
  • Supply-chain change traceability

Best Practices for CCB Operation (2026)

CCB Structure & Cadence

  • Include cross-functional members (Dev, Ops, Sec, Compliance, Business)
  • Hold regular meetings (weekly normal changes, daily emergency)
  • Use emergency CAB (ECAB) for urgent changes

Risk & Decision Making

  • Standardize risk/impact scoring & templates
  • Require rollback/contingency plans for high-risk changes
  • Automate low-risk/standard changes

Post-Change & Continuous Improvement

  • Mandatory PIR for all significant changes
  • Track change-related incidents & root causes
  • Review CCB effectiveness quarterly

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion CCB as core change governance body
  • Approve CCB charter, membership & escalation paths
  • Ensure automation & policy-as-code integration
  • Oversee change success rate, incidents & compliance
  • Monitor CCB cadence, risk scoring & post-change reviews
  • Drive alignment with zero-trust, regulatory & business objectives
  • Integrate CCB with DevOps, ITSM & risk management

ISO/IEC 27018

ISO/IEC 27018 – code of practice for protection of personally identifiable information (PII) in public clouds (2019 edition)

Overview

ISO/IEC 27018:2019 is the international code of practice specifically addressing the protection of personally identifiable information (PII) processed by public cloud service providers. It extends ISO/IEC 27002 with 14 cloud-specific controls and implementation guidance for both cloud service providers and cloud service customers, emphasizing transparency, accountability, data minimization, customer control, and sub-processor management. In March 2026, ISO/IEC 27018 remains the globally recognized benchmark for PII protection in public clouds, frequently required in vendor contracts, data processing agreements (DPAs), privacy impact assessments (PIAs/DPIAs), and compliance frameworks (GDPR Art. 28, CCPA/CPRA, LGPD, PDPA, APPI, NIS2). Major cloud providers (AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle, Alibaba) publish ISO 27018 compliance attestations and mappings to demonstrate adherence.

Key Value: ISO/IEC 27018 provides a clear, auditable framework for protecting PII in public clouds, clarifies shared responsibilities, reduces privacy-related risks, supports secure cloud adoption, and helps demonstrate due diligence to regulators, auditors, customers, and partners.

Core Cloud-Specific Controls (2019 Edition – 2026)

Transparency & Customer Control

  • A.5.1.1 – Provider informs customer of legal obligations affecting PII
  • A.6.1.1 – Customer consent for sub-processor use
  • A.10.1.1 – Customer audit rights & provider cooperation

Data Protection & Segregation

  • A.8.2.1 – PII segregation & isolation in multi-tenant environments
  • A.9.2.1 – Data retention & destruction at contract end
  • A.12.3.1 – Data portability & return/destruction obligations

Incident & Breach Management

  • A.16.1.1 – Provider notification of PII breaches to customer
  • A.18.1.1 – Data location & transfer controls
  • A.18.2.1 – Provider compliance with customer legal requirements

Integration with ISO 27001/27002

  • Extends ISO 27002 controls with PII/cloud-specific guidance
  • Supports joint certification (27001 + 27018)
  • Used in Statement of Applicability (SoA) for cloud services

Key Adoption & Trends in 2026

Cloud Provider Compliance

  • AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle publish ISO 27018 attestations
  • Increasing contractual commitments in DPAs & SLAs
  • Customer demand for 27018 in cloud RFPs & third-party risk

Regulatory & Framework Alignment

  • Strong mapping to GDPR Art. 28 & Schrems II requirements
  • Referenced in CCPA/CPRA, LGPD, PDPA, APPI compliance
  • Supports cross-border data transfer assessments

Emerging Cloud Privacy Risks

  • Guidance for AI/ML workloads processing PII
  • Integration with Zero Trust & multi-cloud governance
  • Focus on sub-processor transparency & consent

Best Practices for ISO/IEC 27018 Adoption (2026)

Cloud Vendor Selection

  • Require ISO 27018 certification or equivalent mapping
  • Include 27018 controls in DPAs & audit rights
  • Verify sub-processor transparency & customer consent mechanisms

Internal Governance

  • Conduct PII-specific risk assessments for cloud workloads
  • Implement customer-controlled encryption & key management
  • Monitor provider compliance reports & incident notifications

Continuous Assurance

  • Regularly review provider attestations & mappings
  • Perform joint audits or third-party assessments
  • Update controls for emerging cloud privacy risks (AI, multi-cloud)

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion ISO/IEC 27018 as core cloud PII protection standard
  • Approve cloud vendor controls & contractual commitments
  • Ensure PII risk assessments & shared responsibility clarity
  • Oversee compliance mapping (GDPR, CCPA, LGPD, etc.)
  • Monitor provider attestations & incident notifications
  • Drive Zero Trust & encryption adoption in cloud
  • Plan for AI/ML workloads & multi-cloud governance

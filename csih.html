CSiH

Certified Security Incident Handler – professional certification for cybersecurity incident detection, analysis, and response

Overview

The Certified Security Incident Handler (CSiH) is a professional certification for IT security and operations staff responsible for detecting, managing, and resolving cybersecurity incidents. It provides structured training in incident response methodology, evidence handling, containment strategies, and post-incident recovery — equipping practitioners to manage incidents effectively across diverse threat scenarios including malware, insider threats, DDoS, and data breaches.

Key Value: CSiH validates the practical incident handling skills that organisations depend on to limit the impact of cybersecurity events — ensuring that incident responders follow consistent, legally defensible, and technically effective processes.

Core Competency Areas

Incident Response Framework

  • Apply the NIST SP 800-61 and SANS incident response lifecycle — preparation, identification, containment, eradication, recovery, and lessons learned
  • Establish and operate an Incident Response Team (IRT) with defined roles, authority, and communication protocols
  • Develop and maintain incident response playbooks for common threat scenarios
  • Apply triage processes to classify, prioritise, and escalate incidents by severity and business impact

Detection & Analysis

  • Identify indicators of compromise (IOCs) across network, endpoint, and application layers
  • Analyse SIEM alerts, EDR telemetry, and log sources to reconstruct incident timelines
  • Apply threat intelligence to contextualise incidents and attribute threat actor activity
  • Distinguish between false positives and genuine security events through structured analysis

Containment, Eradication & Recovery

  • Apply short-term and long-term containment strategies appropriate to the incident type
  • Remove threat actor presence — malware, persistence mechanisms, and compromised credentials
  • Restore systems and services to a known-good state with integrity verification
  • Coordinate recovery activities with IT operations, business continuity, and communications teams

Evidence Handling & Post-Incident Review

  • Preserve and document digital evidence following forensically sound procedures
  • Produce incident reports with timeline, root cause analysis, and remediation recommendations
  • Conduct post-incident reviews to identify lessons learned and improve detection and response
  • Report incidents to management, regulators, and affected parties as required

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Require CSiH for security engineers, SOC analysts, and IT operations staff with incident response responsibilities
  • Ensure the organisation maintains a documented and tested incident response capability at CSiH standards
  • Coordinate with legal, HR, and communications teams to ensure incident response procedures are integrated
  • Use CSiH post-incident review processes to drive continuous improvement in detection and response

Resources

  • SANS Incident Response Resources – SANS FOR508 and FOR572 courses aligned with CSiH competencies
  • CREST Incident Response Standard – UK incident response professional standard
  • NCSC Cyber Incident Response – UK government guidance on managing and reporting cyber incidents
  • EC-Council ECIH – peer EC-Council incident handling certification for comparison and progression

GCTI

GIAC Cyber Threat Intelligence – SANS/GIAC certification for strategic and operational cyber threat intelligence analysis

Overview

The GIAC Cyber Threat Intelligence (GCTI) certification is awarded by GIAC and is based on the SANS FOR578 Cyber Threat Intelligence course. It validates skills in collecting, analysing, and applying threat intelligence to improve defensive operations — covering intelligence cycle management, threat actor profiling, malware analysis, and the production of intelligence products for diverse stakeholder audiences.

Key Value: GCTI is the most technically rigorous threat intelligence certification available — produced by SANS and covering structured analytic techniques, threat actor attribution, and intelligence-driven security operations at a depth that enables professionals to produce high-quality, actionable intelligence products.

Core Competency Areas

Intelligence Lifecycle Management

  • Apply the intelligence cycle — planning, collection, processing, analysis, production, and dissemination
  • Define priority intelligence requirements (PIRs) aligned to organisational threat profile and risk appetite
  • Manage intelligence source reliability, information credibility, and confidence levels
  • Operate threat intelligence platforms (TIPs) — MISP, ThreatConnect, and Anomali

Threat Actor Analysis

  • Profile threat actors using Diamond Model analysis and MITRE ATT&CK TTP mapping
  • Apply structured analytic techniques (ACH, structured brainstorming) to reduce analytical bias
  • Conduct attribution analysis using technical and non-technical indicators
  • Track threat actor campaigns, infrastructure reuse, and tooling evolution over time

Malware & Technical Intelligence

  • Perform malware triage and behavioural analysis to extract IOCs and TTPs
  • Analyse network-based malware indicators — C2 infrastructure, beaconing patterns, and DNS
  • Extract and normalise intelligence in STIX/TAXII format for sharing and tooling integration
  • Correlate technical indicators across incidents to identify campaign patterns

Intelligence-Driven Operations

  • Integrate threat intelligence into SIEM detection rules, vulnerability priorities, and SOC workflows
  • Produce strategic intelligence reports for executives and tactical reports for security operations
  • Participate in ISAC sharing and bilateral intelligence exchange with peer organisations and government

Exam Details

  • 75 questions over 2 hours — open-book (notes permitted)
  • Minimum passing score: 66%
  • Based on SANS FOR578 Cyber Threat Intelligence
  • Certification valid for 4 years; renewed via GIAC CPE credits

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Deploy GCTI-certified analysts in threat intelligence, SOC, and strategic risk functions
  • Establish a threat intelligence programme with GCTI-qualified professionals leading PIR definition and production
  • Use GCTI intelligence products to inform risk reporting, security investment, and board-level threat briefings
  • Integrate GCTI-standard intelligence sharing into ISAC membership and national cyber partnerships

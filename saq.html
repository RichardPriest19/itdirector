SAQ (Self-Assessed Questionnaire)

Structured questionnaire used by organizations to evaluate a vendor’s, partner’s, or internal team’s security, compliance, and risk posture through self-reported answers

Overview

A Self-Assessed Questionnaire (SAQ) is a standardized tool used in third-party risk management (TPRM), vendor due diligence, and compliance programs. It allows suppliers or internal departments to self-report their controls, policies, and practices against a defined framework (e.g., SIG, ISO 27001, NIST, PCI DSS, or custom questionnaires).

Common Types of SAQs

  • SIG (Standardized Information Gathering) – Most widely used comprehensive questionnaire
  • PCI DSS SAQ – Specific self-assessment for payment card data environments
  • ISO 27001 SAQ – Focused on information security management systems
  • NIST-based or custom risk questionnaires
  • Cloud Security Alliance CAIQ – For cloud service providers

Key Purposes

  • Third-party risk assessment and onboarding
  • Ongoing vendor monitoring and re-assessment
  • Regulatory compliance evidence collection
  • Internal control self-assessment (e.g., SOX, SOC 2 readiness)
  • Gap analysis and improvement planning

Best Practices for IT Directors (2026)

  • Automate SAQ distribution and responses using GRC or TPRM platforms
  • Validate self-reported answers with evidence requests or independent audits
  • Score and risk-rate responses with weighted risk models
  • Integrate SAQ results into broader risk registers and dashboards
  • Establish clear remediation timelines for identified gaps

Relation to Other Frameworks

SAQs are frequently used alongside SOC 2 reports, ISO 27001 certifications, and penetration test results. They serve as the first line of defense in vendor risk management programs but should never be the only control.

CDP – Certified Data Professional

ICCP Certified Data Professional – vendor-neutral certification validating data management, governance, and analytics competency

Overview

The Certified Data Professional (CDP) is a professional certification awarded by the Institute for Certification of Computing Professionals (ICCP). It is designed for data management, data governance, analytics, and information management professionals who work across the full data lifecycle. The CDP is vendor-neutral and broadly recognised across industries as a benchmark of data competency at an enterprise level.

Key Value: CDP validates a professional's ability to manage, govern, and leverage data as a strategic organisational asset — covering data architecture, quality, governance, warehousing, and analytics in a single, comprehensive credential.

Core Competency Areas

Data Governance & Stewardship

  • Define and implement data governance frameworks, policies, and standards
  • Assign data ownership, stewardship roles, and accountability structures
  • Align data governance with regulatory requirements (GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA)
  • Develop data quality rules, metrics, and remediation workflows

Data Architecture & Modelling

  • Design logical, physical, and conceptual data models
  • Apply normalisation, dimensional modelling, and data vault techniques
  • Architect data warehouses, data lakes, and hybrid data platforms
  • Define enterprise data standards, taxonomies, and metadata frameworks

Data Management & Quality

  • Implement master data management (MDM) and reference data programmes
  • Profile, cleanse, and enrich data to meet quality thresholds
  • Manage data lineage, cataloguing, and provenance tracking
  • Apply data lifecycle management — creation, storage, archiving, and disposal

Analytics & Business Intelligence

  • Support descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive analytics use cases
  • Enable BI reporting and self-service analytics through governed data products
  • Apply statistical methods and data visualisation best practices
  • Understand AI/ML data requirements — feature engineering, training data quality, and model governance

Certification Requirements & Process

Eligibility & Prerequisites

  • Minimum of two years' experience in a data-related professional role
  • Demonstrated competency across multiple data management domains
  • Agreement to the ICCP Code of Ethics
  • Membership of ICCP or an affiliated professional society recommended

Examination Format

  • Candidates select specialist exam modules aligned to their area of practice (e.g. Data Governance, Data Warehousing, Analytics)
  • Core examination covers foundational data management principles applicable across all specialisms
  • Exams are computer-based and may be taken at authorised testing centres or online
  • Passing scores must be achieved across all selected modules to attain CDP status

Renewal & Continuing Education

  • CDP requires renewal every three years to maintain active certification
  • Continuing professional development (CPD) credits must be logged and submitted
  • Eligible CPD activities include training courses, conferences, publications, and speaking engagements
  • Re-examination is required if the renewal window lapses

Key Trends & Best Practices in 2026

Data Mesh & Decentralised Ownership

  • Shift towards domain-oriented data ownership and federated governance models
  • Implement data products with defined SLAs, discoverability, and interoperability
  • Balance domain autonomy with enterprise data standards and policy compliance

AI-Ready Data Foundations

  • Ensure data quality, completeness, and bias mitigation for AI/ML training datasets
  • Implement AI governance frameworks covering data lineage and model explainability
  • Build feature stores and governed data pipelines to accelerate ML workflows

Data Privacy & Regulatory Compliance

  • Embed privacy-by-design into data architecture and management practices
  • Apply data minimisation, pseudonymisation, and access control at the data layer
  • Maintain compliance with evolving global privacy regulations through automated policy enforcement

Real-Time & Streaming Data Management

  • Extend data governance practices to streaming and event-driven architectures
  • Apply schema registries and data contracts to ensure streaming data quality
  • Manage data latency, ordering, and consistency in real-time analytics pipelines

CDP vs. Related Certifications

CDP vs. CDMP (DAMA)

  • CDMP (Certified Data Management Professional) from DAMA International is the closest peer credential — both are vendor-neutral and cover the full data management lifecycle
  • CDMP is based on the DAMA-DMBOK framework; CDP uses the ICCP's own competency model
  • Many senior data professionals hold or pursue both to maximise recognition across sectors

CDP vs. Vendor Certifications (Microsoft, AWS, Google)

  • Cloud vendor data certifications (e.g. Azure Data Engineer, AWS Data Analytics) are platform-specific
  • CDP provides vendor-neutral credibility applicable across any technology stack or organisation type
  • The two are complementary — CDP validates methodology and governance; vendor certs validate platform implementation

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion the CDP as a professional standard for data architects, engineers, and governance leads
  • Incorporate CDP into role profiles and career pathways for data-focused IT staff
  • Sponsor CDP training and examination costs as part of the IT learning and development budget
  • Use CDP-qualified staff to lead data governance, MDM, and data quality programmes
  • Align CDP competency areas with the organisation's data strategy and regulatory obligations
  • Ensure data management practices meet the standards expected of a CDP-governed function

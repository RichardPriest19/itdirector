CRMP

Certified Risk Management Professional – professional certification for enterprise risk management practitioners

Overview

The Certified Risk Management Professional (CRMP) is a professional designation awarded by the Risk and Insurance Management Society (RIMS) and is designed for risk management practitioners operating across enterprise, operational, technology, and strategic risk domains. It validates the ability to identify, assess, monitor, and mitigate risks at an enterprise level, integrating risk management with organisational decision-making and business continuity.

Key Value: CRMP is the premier enterprise risk management credential in the risk profession — recognised across insurance, financial services, government, and corporate sectors as demonstrating advanced competency in risk strategy, quantification, and governance.

Core Competency Areas

Enterprise Risk Management

  • Apply ERM frameworks — ISO 31000, COSO ERM, and RIMS Risk Maturity Model
  • Develop and implement enterprise-wide risk management programmes and risk appetite statements
  • Integrate ERM into strategic planning, business objectives, and performance management
  • Establish risk governance structures including risk committees and escalation frameworks

Risk Identification & Assessment

  • Apply qualitative and quantitative risk assessment methodologies
  • Conduct risk workshops, scenario analysis, and horizon scanning exercises
  • Maintain a comprehensive enterprise risk register with owners, ratings, and treatment status
  • Assess emerging risks — cyber, geopolitical, climate, and technology disruption

Risk Treatment & Control

  • Design and implement risk mitigation strategies appropriate to risk type and severity
  • Evaluate insurance, contractual transfer, and hedging as risk financing mechanisms
  • Monitor control effectiveness and risk indicators through KRI frameworks
  • Integrate risk management into project management, procurement, and supply chain processes

Risk Reporting & Communication

  • Produce risk reports for boards, executives, and audit committees
  • Develop risk dashboards, heat maps, and bow-tie analyses for stakeholder communication
  • Translate technical risk language into business impact terms for non-specialist audiences

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Engage CRMP-qualified professionals to lead enterprise IT risk management and technology risk programmes
  • Align IT risk management practices with the organisation’s ERM framework and CRMP standards
  • Ensure IT-specific risks (cyber, data, system resilience) are represented in the enterprise risk register
  • Collaborate with CRMP-qualified risk managers to communicate IT risk in business terms to senior leadership

ARM

Application Resource Management – optimising IT resources, applications, and workloads

Overview

Application Resource Management (ARM) is the process of monitoring, optimising, and allocating IT resources to ensure that applications and services run efficiently and cost-effectively. In March 2026, ARM platforms help organisations maximise performance, reduce costs, and maintain compliance by providing visibility into CPU, memory, storage, network usage, and licensing across on-premise, hybrid, and cloud environments. ARM supports IT decision-making by balancing workloads, prioritising critical applications, and forecasting resource needs.

Key Value: ARM ensures optimal utilisation of IT resources, reduces operational costs, improves application performance, and supports strategic IT planning — while maintaining compliance, reducing risks, and aligning IT operations with business priorities.

Core Capabilities (2026)

Resource Monitoring & Allocation

  • Real-time monitoring of CPU, memory, storage, and network usage
  • Automated workload balancing and resource allocation
  • Alerting for resource contention or performance degradation

Application Performance Management

  • End-to-end visibility of application health and performance
  • Identification of performance bottlenecks
  • Correlation of resource usage with application behaviour

Capacity Planning & Forecasting

  • Predictive analytics for resource demand and growth planning
  • Scenario modelling for cloud migration and workload scaling
  • Optimisation of licensing and IT spend

Automation & Integration

  • Automated provisioning, scaling, and decommissioning of resources
  • Integration with ITSM, DevOps, and cloud platforms
  • Policy-based resource management for compliance and efficiency

Key Trends & Best Practices in 2026

Cloud & Hybrid Resource Optimisation

  • Dynamic scaling of workloads across cloud and on-prem environments
  • Cost optimisation for multi-cloud deployments
  • Monitoring of containerised and microservices-based applications

AI-Driven Performance Management

Governance & Compliance

  • Resource usage audits and reporting

  • Risk assessment and mitigation for critical applications

Best Practices for ARM Implementation (2026)

Assessment & Strategy

Deployment & Integration

Monitoring & Optimisation

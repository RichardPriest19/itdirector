ISO/IEC 18000 series

ISO/IEC 18000 – family of standards for radio frequency identification (RFID) air interface protocols for item management

Overview

The ISO/IEC 18000 series defines air interface communications for radio frequency identification (RFID) devices used in item management applications. It specifies parameters for passive and active RFID tags/readers across different frequency bands (LF, HF, UHF). In March 2026, the series remains the global baseline for RFID interoperability, with **ISO/IEC 18000-63** (UHF Type C, aligned with EPCglobal Gen2v2) as the dominant standard for supply chain, retail, logistics, and asset tracking. Recent additions include **ISO/IEC 18000-65:2026** (streaming sensors based on 18000-63). The family supports passive backscatter, active RFID, and emerging IoT/sensor use cases, with strong adoption in manufacturing, retail, healthcare, defense, and global supply chains. It is frequently referenced in GS1 EPC standards, regulatory traceability requirements, and compliance frameworks (e.g., FDA, EU traceability rules).

Key Value: The ISO/IEC 18000 series provides standardized, interoperable air interface protocols for RFID — enabling global supply chain visibility, asset tracking, inventory accuracy, anti-counterfeiting, and IoT connectivity while ensuring vendor-neutral, long-term compatibility and regulatory compliance.

Core Parts & Protocols (2026)

Key Active Parts

  • ISO/IEC 18000-63 – Parameters for air interface at 860–960 MHz Type C (UHF, EPC Gen2v2 base)
  • ISO/IEC 18000-6 – Parameters for air interface at 860–930 MHz (UHF general)
  • ISO/IEC 18000-65:2026 – Parameters for streaming sensors based on 18000-63
  • ISO/IEC 18000-3 – Parameters for air interface at 13.56 MHz (HF)
  • ISO/IEC 18000-2 – Parameters for air interface at <135 kHz (LF)

Main Characteristics

  • Passive backscatter (most common) & active RFID support
  • Multi-tag reading & anti-collision protocols
  • Security features (authentication, encryption in some parts)
  • Global frequency harmonization (860–960 MHz UHF dominant)

Integration & Alignment

  • Aligned with GS1 EPCglobal Gen2v2
  • Supports traceability (FDA, EU food/pharma regulations)
  • Used with ISO 18000-65 for sensor-enabled RFID

Key Adoption & Trends in 2026

Supply Chain & Retail

  • Dominant UHF standard (18000-63) for item-level tagging
  • GS1 EPC Gen2v2 interoperability
  • Retail & logistics traceability mandates

IoT & Sensor Integration

  • 18000-65:2026 for streaming sensor applications
  • Passive RFID + sensor fusion in IoT
  • Edge computing & real-time data

Security & Compliance

  • Authentication & encryption enhancements
  • Compliance with GDPR, NIS2, FDA DSCSA
  • Supply chain security & anti-counterfeiting

FIRM (Financial Institution Resilience Management)

Framework and capability for ensuring financial resilience of banks and financial institutions against operational disruptions, cyber threats, and ICT risks

Overview

FIRM stands for Financial Institution Resilience Management. It is a structured approach (strongly aligned with DORA in the EU and similar regulatory expectations globally) that helps financial organizations build, test, and maintain resilience against severe operational disruptions, including cyber attacks, ICT failures, and third-party risks.

Core Components

  • ICT Risk Management Framework
  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA) and Critical Function Identification
  • Resilience Testing (including threat-led penetration testing and scenario-based exercises)
  • Incident Response and Recovery Planning
  • Third-Party / Supply Chain Risk Oversight
  • Continuous Monitoring and Reporting to Regulators

Regulatory Context (2026)

FIRM is heavily driven by the EU’s **Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA)**, which mandates financial entities to demonstrate ICT resilience. Similar expectations exist in the UK (FCA/PRA), US (FFIEC), and other jurisdictions. It goes beyond traditional Business Continuity (BCM) by emphasizing proactive testing and digital resilience.

Why It Matters for IT Directors

FIRM requires close collaboration between IT, Risk, Compliance, and Business teams. IT leaders are responsible for implementing resilient architectures, secure cloud strategies, robust incident response, and measurable recovery capabilities (RTO/RPO) for critical systems.

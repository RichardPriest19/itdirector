UAT (User Acceptance Testing)

Final phase of testing where end users validate that a system, application, or solution meets business requirements and is ready for production deployment

Overview

User Acceptance Testing (UAT) is the last formal testing stage before go-live. It is performed by actual business users (not the development or QA team) to confirm that the delivered solution fulfills the original business needs, workflows, and expectations in a real-world environment.

Key Objectives of UAT

  • Verify that the system meets documented business and functional requirements
  • Ensure the solution is intuitive and supports day-to-day user workflows
  • Identify any gaps, usability issues, or missing functionality
  • Build user confidence and ownership before go-live
  • Provide formal sign-off for production deployment

Typical UAT Process

  1. Define UAT scope, scenarios, and success criteria
  2. Prepare test data and environment (ideally production-like)
  3. Train users and provide test scripts/scenarios
  4. Execute testing and log defects
  5. Prioritize and resolve issues (usually through a defect triage process)
  6. Obtain formal business sign-off

Best Practices for IT Directors

  • Involve business users early in requirements gathering
  • Ensure a separate UAT environment that closely mirrors production
  • Use real or realistic test data (with proper anonymization)
  • Track defects in a central tool with clear severity and ownership
  • Define clear exit criteria and rollback plan
  • Document UAT results for audit and compliance purposes

Relation to Other Testing Phases

UAT comes after:

  • Unit Testing (developers)
  • Integration Testing
  • System Testing / QA Testing
It is the final validation from the business perspective before moving to production.

DSPT

Data Security and Privacy Toolkit – NHS mandatory self-assessment for data security & privacy standards

Overview

The Data Security and Privacy Toolkit (DSPT) is the mandatory annual self-assessment tool published by NHS England to help NHS organisations, primary care, and commissioned partners demonstrate compliance with the **National Data Guardian's 10 data security standards** and key legal requirements (UK GDPR, Data Protection Act 2018, Caldicott Principles, common law duty of confidentiality). The current version is **DSPT 2025/26** (live since April 2025), with increased focus on **AI/ML data risks**, **cyber resilience**, **third-party assurance**, **cloud security**, **incident reporting maturity**, **patient/public trust**, and **evidence-based assertions**. In March 2026, DSPT submissions are required by 30 June 2026, with organisations asserting compliance against 10 standards and providing evidence (policies, risk assessments, training records, audit logs). It is mandatory for NHS trusts, ICBs, GP practices, and many commissioned partners, with public visibility of assertions and growing regulatory scrutiny (NHS Digital oversight, ICO alignment).

Key Value: DSPT provides a structured, evidence-based self-assessment framework to demonstrate data security and privacy maturity — ensuring patient trust, regulatory compliance (UK GDPR, Caldicott), cyber resilience, and continuous improvement while enabling transparent reporting and benchmarking across the NHS and partner organisations.

Core 10 Data Security Standards (2025/26 – 2026)

1. Personal confidential data is protected

  • Appropriate technical & organisational measures
  • Encryption, access controls, pseudonymisation

2. Staff are trained and aware

  • Mandatory data security & privacy training
  • Annual refresh & role-based awareness

3. Personal confidential data is only accessed by authorised staff

  • RBAC, least privilege, regular access reviews
  • Audit logging & monitoring

4. Personal confidential data is used lawfully

  • Lawful basis, DPIA, Caldicott approval
  • Transparency & patient choice
5–10 (summary)
  • 5. Robust IT systems & processes
  • 6. Safe sharing of personal confidential data
  • 7. Personal confidential data is kept safe when transferred
  • 8. Personal confidential data is kept securely when no longer needed
  • 9. Incidents are managed effectively
  • 10. Organisations demonstrate accountability & continuous improvement

Key Trends & Requirements in 2026

Submission & Evidence

  • Annual submission deadline: 30 June
  • Evidence-based assertions (Level 0–3 maturity)
  • Public assertion publication & NHS oversight

Emerging Focus Areas

  • AI/ML data processing & DPIA requirements
  • Cyber resilience & third-party assurance
  • Cloud security & secure data environments
  • Patient/public trust & transparency

Regulatory & Alignment

  • Strong mapping to UK GDPR, Data Protection Act 2018
  • Alignment with Caldicott Principles, NHS Data Strategy
  • Reference in Data Security & Protection Toolkit audits

Best Practices for DSPT Adoption (2026)

Preparation & Submission

  • Start early – evidence collection from April
  • Use DSPT evidence templates & guidance
  • Engage Caldicott Guardian & DPO early

Governance & Evidence

  • Maintain central evidence repository
  • Conduct internal mock assessments
  • Document AI/ML & cloud DPIAs

Continuous Improvement

  • Address gaps post-submission
  • Integrate with incident & change processes
  • Update for emerging risks (AI, cyber resilience)

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Champion DSPT as core NHS data security & privacy governance tool
  • Approve DSPT submission & evidence strategy
  • Ensure Caldicott Guardian, DPO & SIRO involvement
  • Oversee evidence collection, DPIAs & gap remediation
  • Monitor DSPT maturity, incident reporting & compliance
  • Drive AI/ML & cloud risk assessments
  • Align DSPT with UK GDPR, ISO 27001 & cyber resilience

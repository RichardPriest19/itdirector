RASCI

RASCI Matrix – Responsibility Assignment Chart (Responsible, Accountable, Support, Consulted, Informed) for clear role definition in projects, processes, and operations

Overview

The RASCI matrix is a responsibility assignment tool used to clarify roles and responsibilities across tasks, deliverables, or processes. It is an enhanced version of the traditional RACI model, adding a "Support" role for practical team collaboration in complex IT and business environments.

RASCI Definitions

  • Responsible (R) – Does the work / executes the task
  • Accountable (A) – Ultimately answerable for the task completion and quality (only one per task)
  • Support (S) – Provides resources, assistance, or additional hands to help complete the task
  • Consulted (C) – Must be consulted before decision or action (two-way communication)
  • Informed (I) – Kept up-to-date on progress or outcome (one-way communication)

Best Practices 2026

  • Use RASCI during project initiation, process improvement, and organizational change initiatives
  • Keep the matrix simple, visible, and living – review it regularly in governance meetings
  • Ensure only one Accountable (A) per task to avoid diffusion of responsibility
  • Combine with RICE/MoSCoW prioritization and project stage gates for stronger governance
  • Document RASCI in project charters, SOWs, and IT governance frameworks (COBIT, ITIL)
  • Integrate into tools like Jira, Confluence, Microsoft Planner, or Power Apps for dynamic tracking

CISA

Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency – U.S. civilian agency for cyber defense & critical infrastructure protection

Overview

The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) is the United States' lead civilian agency for cybersecurity and critical infrastructure protection, operating under the Department of Homeland Security (DHS). In March 2026, CISA is the central coordinator for national cyber defense — issuing **alerts**, **binding directives**, **vulnerability guidance**, **Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog**, **Shields Up** campaigns, **ransomware disruption operations**, **supply-chain security requirements**, **Secure by Design** mandates, **AI-enabled cyber threat intelligence**, and **cross-sector coordination** through the Joint Cyber Defense Collaborative (JCDC). It enforces key regulations (Cyber Incident Reporting for Critical Infrastructure Act, CISA Directives), runs the **Vulnerability Disclosure Policy**, and partners globally (Five Eyes, EU, NATO CCDCOE). CISA is increasingly focused on **operational collaboration**, **zero-trust adoption**, **quantum-resistant cryptography**, and **critical infrastructure resilience** against nation-state and ransomware threats.

Key Value: CISA provides authoritative threat intelligence, actionable guidance, regulatory enforcement, and public-private coordination — helping organizations reduce cyber risk, respond to incidents faster, comply with mandatory reporting, harden critical infrastructure, and — through JCDC & KEV — defend against the most active threats facing the nation and its allies.

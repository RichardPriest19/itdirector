CSOC

Certified SOC Analyst – EC-Council certification for Security Operations Centre analysts and threat monitoring professionals

Overview

The Certified SOC Analyst (C|SA / CSOC) is awarded by EC-Council and is designed for entry-to-intermediate level Security Operations Centre (SOC) analysts. It covers the knowledge and skills required to work within a SOC environment — monitoring, detecting, analysing, and responding to cybersecurity threats and incidents using SIEM, log analysis, and threat intelligence tools.

Key Value: CSOC is the foundational professional standard for SOC analysts — providing structured knowledge of SOC workflows, threat monitoring, incident triage, and escalation procedures that equip professionals to contribute effectively from day one in a SOC environment.

Core Exam Domains

SOC Fundamentals

  • Understand SOC tiers, roles, responsibilities, and operational workflows
  • Apply the incident response lifecycle — preparation, detection, analysis, containment, and recovery
  • Work with ticketing systems, escalation procedures, and shift handover protocols
  • Apply SOC metrics and KPIs to measure detection, response, and resolution effectiveness

SIEM & Log Management

  • Operate SIEM platforms including Splunk, IBM QRadar, and Microsoft Sentinel
  • Collect, normalise, and correlate log data from endpoints, networks, and applications
  • Build and tune SIEM correlation rules, alerts, and use cases to reduce false positives
  • Investigate SIEM alerts and reconstruct attack timelines from log evidence

Threat Intelligence & Detection

  • Integrate threat intelligence feeds (STIX/TAXII) into SOC detection and response workflows
  • Apply MITRE ATT&CK framework to map adversary techniques and improve detection coverage
  • Perform indicator of compromise (IOC) analysis and threat hunting using available tooling
  • Triage and escalate incidents based on severity, impact, and business context

Network & Endpoint Monitoring

  • Monitor network traffic using IDS/IPS, NetFlow, and packet capture analysis
  • Analyse endpoint telemetry using EDR platforms for threat detection
  • Identify anomalous behaviour patterns indicative of malware, insider threat, or data exfiltration

Exam Details

  • Exam code: 312-39 — 100 multiple-choice questions over 3 hours
  • Recommended prerequisite: 1 year of IT security or networking experience
  • Renewal: 120 EC-Council Continuing Education (ECE) credits every 3 years
  • Provides foundation for progression to CEH, CHFI, and EC-Council’s higher-tier certifications

IT Director Responsibilities

  • Require CSOC for SOC Tier 1 and Tier 2 analyst appointments
  • Use CSOC training as the structured onboarding pathway for new SOC team members
  • Ensure SOC analyst skills are maintained through CPD and progression to higher-level certifications
  • Align SOC workflows, runbooks, and tooling with CSOC competency standards

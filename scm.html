SCM

Supply Chain Management – planning, sourcing, manufacturing, and logistics optimisation

Overview

Supply Chain Management (SCM) encompasses the end-to-end coordination of sourcing, procurement, production, inventory, logistics, and distribution. In March 2026, SCM is highly digitalised, leveraging cloud platforms, real-time data, AI-driven forecasting, IoT tracking, and advanced analytics to optimise operations. Modern SCM integrates tightly with ERP systems, eCommerce platforms, and logistics providers to deliver visibility, resilience, and efficiency across global supply networks. It plays a critical role in cost control, customer satisfaction, and business continuity.

Key Value: SCM enables organisations to optimise the flow of goods, information, and finances — improving efficiency, reducing costs, increasing resilience, and delivering better customer outcomes through end-to-end visibility and control.

Best Practices for SCM Implementation (2026)

Technology & Integration
